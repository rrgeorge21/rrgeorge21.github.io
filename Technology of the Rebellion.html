TECHNOLOGY OF THE REBELLION​

This thread outlines common vessels, weapons, and other technological creations for use in the role-play by Rebel forces. It is also an important reference for vocabulary, force strength, and fleet capabilities. Newly created items (via role-play or discussion) will be added as the story progresses.

The Rebel Militia possesses an odd assortment of starships, vehicles, and equipment, most of which were formerly in service to the Galactic Federation of Free Alliances or brought in by allies and defectors. The Imperial Knights, Defiance, and other organizations that make up the armed forces of the Rebellion often rely upon privately-owned starships and equipment more than anything, though frequent raids on Imperial supply lines have given them the means to supply arms and armor to many of their under-equipped militias.

Constructed as a complement to the Star Defender after the Siege of Manaan, the Defiance is intended to be the heavy hitter to the MC200's bulwark. Built for raw firepower, the ship falls apart in some areas compared to its more defense minded cousin, but makes up for it in the amount of fire it can bring to bear. The ship features a great many heavy turbolaser cannons, not bothering with any special variants, or fancy super powered designs. It features a much more advance sensor and targeting system, furthering the ability of its lighter armament to deal with swarms of fighters, though it doesn't bother with pointless close in systems, instead sticking with a lot more tried and true heavy gun designs to take out the heavily armored Imperium designs. The Defiance makes use of a Mon Calamari deflector shield arrangement with a large amount of smaller generators inside the vessel as opposed to the standard, allowing for a redundant system to be easily installed, and making it harder to disable the generators in the first place. Though somewhat lighter than the Star Defender, it still provides more than adequate defenses to let the destroyer slug it out.

For the most part, the ship is simplicity incarnate. Learning from the lessons of the Siege of Manaan, it was designed under the belief that any new assault would force the Manaan peoples to make heavy use of fewer designs going up again a more numerous foe. This would in turn put great strain on the shipyards again, diverting resources away from building new vessels, to repairing those taking part in the defense. Thus the Defiance was designed to have a very quick turn around time in repair work, and even without support services, the ship is well equipped to deal with ion cannon fire that it might come up against. Damage control teams are specially outfitted with gear to replace damage to systems caused by ion fire, and the ship features a number of independent power supplies to ensure that things like gravity and life support stay on to allow them to do their work quickly, and unencumbered.

By all official records, the Sphinx was a simple experimental variant of the standard Banehound design becoming common in the Imperium fleet. Featuring a light armament, different hangar layout, and faster engines the Mark XI.x was to be faster cousin to the Mark XI.v to hunt down smugglers more efficiently. The project was eventually abandoned, as lighter vessels were chosen to fulfill this role instead, and the Sphinx herself eventually ended up in the hands of the Ubiqorate, who further modified the vessel.

Most notably is the Sphinx's difference in armament layout compared to a standard Banehound. The Super-Heavy tuborlasers are mounted in single ball shaped turrets, with two on the ventral, and dorsal side, and one on the port and starboard running centerline with the rest of the ship. The power settings of the Sphinx's guns are not as variable as those on a standard Banehound, and are instead locked in at the medium level, but make up for it with a capability of 12 shots per a minute, and a full rotation rate of only 17 seconds as opposed to the standard's 8 and 34 respectively. One of the Heavy Dual Turbolasers the ship carries is mounted in the prow, but the remaining guns are mounted across the ventral, and dorsal sides of the ship. The Sphinx has a even distribution of guns, and the spread in effect makes the Sphinx an anti-T crosser. The Star Destroyer can bring all its guns to bear down on a forward target, making the standard t-cross tactic as close to suicide as you can get. The dual light laser cannons are intended for anti-starfighter duties, and are a cut above similar designs in terms of accuracy, and rate of fire, but are somewhat less optimized in terms of how hard they hit, and range.

The variable ordinance launchers have a somewhat odd placement. Two are built into the forward prow centerline with the ship, and facing forward. The other two face rear, with one on the dorsal, and one on the ventral side of the hull that sticks out beyond the central engine unit. The launchers have shutters that close over them, to prevent starfighters from attempting to Death Star the Sphinx.

The hangars are the other significant departure from the standard Banehound, which uses the same basic design as on the first Imperial-classes, with the addition of starfighter launch tubes along the vertical axis. The Sphinx has four hangars, two port and two starboard, which bear a stronger resemblance towards Mon Calamari, and old CIS designs with a large shielded open face, and armored shutters that can be closed. The hangars are not pressurized and have no atmosphere or gravity however, and are heavily armored with thick alusteel plating. Craft enter the ship proper through elevators on the floor or ceiling of the hangar, magnetizing themselves to the appropriate surface. The intent behind the design is to allow ship's that dock to be checked thoroughly for any tracking devices, or explosives, before exposing them to to parts of the ship where such things can do damage. Teams of personnel in spacesuits will comb over the hull of every ship that lands. Fighter launches are facilitated by a carousel design at the center, in between the hangars. The design allows a fighter to be loaded on top, one on bottom, and then both will be launched, exiting via whichever hangar it's rotated into. Twelve of these carousel exist (six in the forward two hangars, six in the rear two) allowing for two squadrons to be launched at a time.

The escape pods of the Sphinx are a more standard design as opposed to the large Skiphawk lifeboats on the standard design, freeing up a lot of room for the additional electronics equipment carried by the Sphinx. In general the interior of the ship follows the same design pattern as the Banehound, with a buried bridge, jettisonable detention rigs, narrow corridors, and so on. The big exception to this is the reactor bulb area. Like the Banehound, the Sphinx uses a wheel shaped design that's buried further into the center. However where the Banehound uses the space freed up in the reactor bulb for additional recreational facilities, the Sphinx uses it to house all the equipment you'd find in a listening post. This allows the Sphinx to quietly monitor communications in the area of space where it is, and sift through the traffic to find coded messages from agents in the mess of everyday traffic.

The crew of the Sphinx is heavily vetted by the Ubiqorate, and the ship observes a strict policy on entrance. Outside technology is not allowed on the ship unless cleared by the Director or the Keeper directly, and accessing the ship is done only through pre-approved means of transport. Operative access codes are disabled when the operative is either on leave (vacation) or stationed at a mobile command center, and codes are scrambled every seven days. Every operative knows not to write their codes down under any circumstances. The ship flies in a random patrol throughout Imperial space, known only to a select group of pilots that rotate on a bi-monthly schedule. Furthermore it makes use of an ST Box that allows the Sphinx to present itself as the ISD Janus, a standard Banehound that actually does exist.

DESCRIPTION​
Designed as a replacement for some of the rushed designs put forward by the Alliance Engineering Corporation during the Core Campaign, the Sanctuary never got a chance to truly shine, or a hit a full production run after the events of the battle of Coruscant. The ship was intended to be something of a light carrier for the Galactic Alliance fleet, with a good but far from stellar armament, a great amount of endurance, and the ability to support a large fighter complement for a vessel of her size. With the primary focus being on endurance, open hangar designs were forgone, as were the armored flight tubes of other vessels that were only capable of fitting certain sized craft. Instead the designers of the Sanctuary drew inspiration from classic ocean going aircraft carrier which featured elevator platforms to bring vessels up to the flight deck. The elevators on the Sanctuary are capable of bring a fully loaded squadron of starfighters up on a single platform apiece. 4 such platforms exist, and in order for ship's to dock they are guided by the Sanctuary's tractor beams onto an appropriate elevator platform. The arrangement also allows for the inspection of vessels by being's in spacesuits for possible problems.

The platforms themselves are made of thick duranium, and the hangars are likewise heavily armored to prevent any explosions that might occur from destroying the entire vessel. Great emphasis is placed on durability, and the command center is buried in the ship's hull, a design feature that's fairly common and in no way unique to a single species or tech firm despite claims to the contrary. The ship makes use of a Mon Calamari deflector shield arrangement with a large amount of smaller generators inside the vessel as opposed to the standard, allowing for a redundant system to be easily installed, and making it harder to disable the generators in the first place. The armament of the Sanctuary is mainly geared towards fending off starfighter, and bomber attacks. The medium turbolasers allow it to go toe-to-toe with other capital ships, but generally against something of its own size, without fighter support, it won't come out winning. The heavy laser cannons, as well as the lighter ones, are effective at shredding incoming fighters however, especially droids, and rookie fighters who have never had to take on a dedicated ASF battery like that the Sanctuary possesses.

A handful of thse ships exist in the hands of independent planetary governments such as the Manaan Alliance, and Bothan Space, and it wouldn't be inconceivable for further more to be produced should blueprints be found, or one be allowed to be taken apart and examined by a sufficiently advanced shipyard.

DESCRIPTION​
The Genesis-class Cruiser was purpose built in the months pre-dating the Battle of Manaan. Constructed to operate as a mobile headquarters and command center for the men and women who would grow to form the Alliance for a New Republic. This one-off vessel, which would be christened the "Rebel Dawn", is a small, yet powerful vessel within its own right. Its box-like design follows the lead of vessels such as the Kushari Federation cruisers, which favour rigid, intricate skeletal designs and additional armour. Having these skeletal structures built using a honeycomb like mesh of titanium and alusteel throughout the superstructure allows the ship to be home to four respectable sized hangar bays. One at the bow of the ship, one on the dorsal side, just aft of the bridge, and two bays on the port and starboard sides located midship.

In addition to the respectable starfighter and shuttle complement the vessel is capable of holding, the ship also is in possession of four pillar-like protrusions in each quarter of the ship's exterior. These pillars are used for atmospheric flight, landing, and submersion. In truth, these pillars are actually high-powered turbines, which when coupled with the vessel's ion engines and inertial thrusters allows it to move seamlessly between aquatic, atmospheric and vacuum environments. Armed with a relatively small outfit of weaponry, the Genesis-class can hold its own in a small skirmish, but its true power lies in its defensive capabilities. Its honeycombed structure, armour plating, and advanced engine systems puts this ship in a much better situation for fast exfiltration. Performing as the operations center for the Rebellion, this is a valuable part of the ship's capabilities.


The ship's interior seems and feels cramped in any of the rooms or corridors. A ceiling height of only 2.3 metres and corridors rarely wider than 110 centimetres allows for more space to contribute to the ship's four hangar bays, and expansive bunk network. Favouring simplicity and function over luxuries, the Genesis-class is capable of holding more equipment, personnel, and rations than other ships of its size.

The ship's interior is laid out in a grid pattern, with two major corridors running the length of the ship, dividing it into three major areas: port, center, and starboard. In the interest of the preservation of life and information, the center area of the ship is reinforced with a redunant life support system which encompasses the areas which include the barracks, mess, infirmary and command deck. Beyond these areas, in the outer areas of the ship lie areas such as the gymnasium, armoury, operation and briefing rooms. Onboard in non-essential areas of the ship are also several information access points.

Worth mentioning is that the fore and aft hangar bays also double as training rooms. The floors, which were originally designed to shield hangar personnel from ion output when craft were flying in and out of the hangar, are completely alternating, and are able to be propped up, down, on their side automatically by actuators underneath the floor. This modularity allows mock-up urban environments to be simulated, providing cover, change in terrain, and can be changed at a whim in the hangar control room that sits above the hangar behind reinforced transparisteel windows.

Despite the cramped interior, the ship serves as a functional, practical, and safe location for Rebel participants to train, relax, or research and plan their next moves against the New Sith Imperium.
