History

Much of the Empire's history leading to its inception is tied up with that of Woade Blite. Blite laid much of the ground work for Krogan advancement, this cannot go without saying. The Rebellions required a new course to take...but Blite's would unfortunately doom Krogan for yet another round of the same destruction as last time. In the end, it was expansionism, the very thing he saw as needed to abate Krogan agression, that did him in. His initial commitment and withdrawal from the Systems Alliance Civil War resulted in Krogan who initially supported him turning against him, some calling for his abdication, others for his head. In 2205, Blite stepped down from the throne and retired.

In his place rose Battlecaptain Urdnot Lorkali. Logical, philosophical, a scholarly warrior taking a strong pulse of the Krogan on domestic policy, though a novice on the international stage. It's now up to this Battlecaptain turned Emperor to lead the Krogan to their future, one that shouldn't result in another Rebellions and their extermination.

Timeline

Approx 4000 years prior to 2183: The Krogan, technologically advanced and having overcome the rigors of living on Tuchanka with its hostile wildlife, began fighting each other. The Krogan began fighting each other. A formerly culturally rich people with architectural and artistic achievements of their own fought over small tracts of land because of an exploding population. Krogan destroy their entire civilization in nuclear hellfire, turning Tuchanka into a vast wasteland. Those with Blood Rage, a mental illness as far as Krogan are concerned, become some of the strongest survivors in this post-apocalyptic world.

1 - 80: The Salarians open an unopened relay and discover the Rachni. This starts the chain of events that leads to the uplifting of the Krogan as the Citadel Council's military force in fighting the Rachni, believing the bugs are hostile beyond redemption. The Krogan are given new technology and worlds in exchange for foot soldiers. The year 80 is when Krogan begin arriving in force and begin taking the fight to the Rachni.

81 - 300: The Krogan brutally fight the Rachni, their ability to reproduce with great haste gives them the edge in a fight against the insect race. Krogan fight the Rachni all the way back to Suen and destroy their hives. This leads to the Citadel government declaring the Rachni extinct by 300 and leaving a listening post for possible survivors. The Krogan are hailed as heroes and a statue on the Presidium is raised in honor of their accomplishments.

301 - 700: Off Tuchanka, Krogan, failing to learn from their past, begin reproducing exponentially, claiming other worlds by force. They even begin taking worlds held by other Council members, each time claiming they needed just "one more world" for living space. One such world is Garvug, a formerly lush planet which the Krogan have ecologically ruined thanks to their poor practices in planetary upkeep. The Krogan then began inhabiting the Asari world of Lusia; the Council refused to hand control of Lusia over to the Krogan and ordered them off the planet. Overlord Kredak, Krogan ambassador to the Council, dared the Council to come take their planets back by force and stormed out of the Council Chambers. This act is considered the start of The Krogan Rebellions.

701 - ~1000s: When the Rebellions ended is a matter of debate, some say as late as the 1000s, some say at ~780 when Parthian forces nuked the Krogan world of Canrum. The Rebellions were the first official action of the Spectres, easily able to replenish their numbers against Asari and Salarian forces. It took the newcomer to the spacefaring community, the Turians, to prove a brutal match. Krogan used total war, just as the Turians did, so when Turians faced asteroid strikes against several of their colonies rendering them uninhabitable, the Hierarchy lashed back in kind. The resulting action led to the development of the Genophage and the distribution in 710, the crippling virus killing their reproductive capability. Unable to replenish their numbers, Krogan increasingly quit fighting in the face of an unsustainable war, leading many to surrender by the mid 700s. Rogue warlords continued to fight in the meantime and were brutally put down by Legion forces, others taking their bands and leaving to the frontiers and becoming pirates. In any event, the war was over, the Krogan lost, and were in a horrific post-war position.

~770s - 2203: With the Genophage, and unable to replenish their numbers as before, the Krogan begin their decline. As time goes on, many Krogan believe that without a cure or some change, it will mean the extinction of their people by the end of the 23rd century. Blood Rage has become so pervasive in Krogan society that those without it start becoming rare, but may still come about. This mental illness sees Krogan more focused on fighting each other and militaristic pursuits rather than focusing on reversing the damage. Intellectuals are sneered at and mocked by society. Even the remaining small numbers of Krogan engaged in sciences and philosophy see their own people as a lost cause, but not everyone gives up.

Culture & Beliefs

The Empire is not too removed culturally from what Krogan are. There are still many unique rites and traditions in each clan that have been practiced over the ages. Many of these have been preserved; there are some which are much more harmful for Krogan longevity that were abolished under the previous regime. Though, it should be noted that Blite's government was extremely skeptical of incoming traditions. Skepticism still remains as to the reasons why, ranging from insulting Blite's ego to undermining a sort of religious "godemperor" cult around his personage.

Lorkali has taken a more muted approach since becoming Emperor, smashing the cult of personality surrounding the Emperor. He allows clans to keep their unique rites and traditions so long as they do not breed conflict with other clans or involve the sacrifice of aliens or other Krogan. This won him some support among a small number of moderately Independent clans who have since opened up negotiations with the Empire. He sees this as a positive forward, albeit a slow step, in preserving Krogan culture while breeding new allies that will hopefully unify. His preference is to deal diplomatically and, in typical respect for Krogan strength, they would come around in due course.

Old and new begin to meet, and Lorkali is trying to move the people away from resorting to a fight first and never questioning it. It's a new direction, not one favored by archtraditionalists, but a valid direction that, as he sees it, will not get the Krogan killed again out of some senseless lust for war.

Imperial Philosophy

Blite laid a foundation for the Empire's philosophy. It's been improved on over the years, especially under Lorkali.
  • There are other paths besides that of a warrior. As a people, Krogan must also place more value and respect into intelligence, philosophy, the study of the past, scholarly pursuits. Krogan fell down a path of self-destruction from lack of knowledge. It must not be repeated.
  • Isolationism, self-centered actions, these are things which get Krogan killed, and a sign of a lack of character. Krogan must unite, stand together, that is where the species has strength. There is power in numbers, and aliens understand this far more than Krogan have!
  • Krogan must adapt, must prosper, putting the needs of the Empire and Clan above their own. If this isn't done? What hope is there of a future?
  • As a species, Krogan need to start seeing each other as kinsmen. Clan pride is all well and good, but if we are to one day achieve unity, we must all stand shoulder to shoulder! How else can we arise from the ashes stronger than before?!
  • Service to the Empire is akin to service to the Clan. Remember both before personal desires, everyone has something they want, but don't put that before what will make us strong as a species.
  • Diplomacy is the first step. It must be done, there is no escaping this. What can be gained by words and cooperation will far exceed that by force of arms.
  • War is the final resort if all avenues fail. Do not seek it. If it comes to us, stand ready to fight with every sinew.
  • In this vein, do not resort to pointless violence. It is the mark of a savage, and Krogan must become anything but that. If we do not advance beyond base brutality, the aliens will not hesitate to exterminate us down to the last man, woman, and whelp as a failed species too dangerous to be left alive. That is the score, keep that in mind, we are zero for one, and two will be our end.

Clan Hierarchy and Assigned Roles

The Emperor's Fist is only as strong as the fingers it is composed of. An individual finger lacks the sheer brutality to strike out against the Empire's adversaries to keep them at bay. Together, in unison, and clenched together tightly with purpose, there is no level of protection in the galaxy that will repel the might of the krogan. Levels of representation in the Empire are determined by many contributing factors. Elements such as clan population, territory controlled, economic contributions, military prowess, and other such variables determined the roles fulfilled within the Imperial order. No clan is heralded as superior to another as even the strongest will fall without allies to strengthen their resolve and supply the front lines.

The Emperor of all Empire-loyal krogan. He is the undisputed ruler, leader of all, a fount of wisdom, ferocity, and commands respect of those beneath him. He is the Chieftain of all krogan, all krogan are his clan, his kinsmen. Any future successors will take on "Krogan" and relieve themselves of their clan in transition through the Rite of Ascension.

High Overlords


Description:
The High Overlord Clans are amongst the largest, more powerful collectives of clans within the Empire hierarchy. They are responsible for a majority of the provisions made to the Empire, such as the conscription of forces, tribute of resources, credits, labor, and all manner of necessities to ensure the Empire's stability. In return, they hold seats of power in the decision-making process and are only subservient to Emperor Blite. Upon their own homeworlds where their clans are powerful to begin with, they assist in regulating their own beneath Empire rule.

Overlord Clans are somewhat smaller than the High Overlord clans, but still respectably powerful in their own right. These clans, given their lower numbers but strength, take priority for special duties during conscription, such as support roles and special forces. This does not limit their conscription from Imperial Troopers, but merely an option to preserve the clans and not deplete their numbers.

Warlord Clans

Description:
Warlord clans are smaller, but still supportive. Very much like in Imperial Philosophy, each must do their part and do so unquestionably in service to the greater scheme of things rather than selfishly for themselves. Warlord Clans assist Overlord Clans in their assigned duties decreed by Imperial Law. Imperial Armed Forces


Like any Empire, the Krogan require a military. It is in their blood, warriors built by centuries of adaptation to the harsh reality of the galaxy. From a homeworld where they are prey, the lowest on the food chain, survival was a day-to-day activity and continues to be so in present times. Conflict is their way of life. While most of the galaxy views them as brutal, pragmatic, and in some cases, unintelligent and undeveloped, the krogan are masters of the art of warfare. They have an affinity, an appreciation for it, and dying in battle against a formidable foe is no greater honor to be bestowed. They live for the fight, desire strong enemies, and wish there is no shortage in the near future.

While times have changed, both literally and metaphorically between the advancement of the galaxy and the Genophage impacting krogan reproduction rates, the Empire desired to keep that aspect of their livelihood alive and well. The Imperial Army harnesses that chaotic aura of a battle-hardened krogan and disciplined it. The average krogan is quite dangerous and difficult to combat. An Imperial Trooper is much of the same, multiplied many times over, and follows orders like a properly structured military. They are not drones, but controlled chaos. A rule-of-thumb that is followed by the regime is the average Imperial Trooper should be capable of besting up to 10 average krogan warriors in combat by themselves, double that for "lesser" races. Such success comes at a price, however.

As decreed by the Imperial Warmaster and forefather of the training regime, each graduating class of Imperial Troopers is allotted up to 20% failure rate to be deemed "acceptable". The current average failure rate has been curbed back to just 10% with supervision from instructing Battlemasters. Injuries are down as well with substantial medical staff on standby. Even krogan can suffer wounds after all. Those who fail to make the grade for infantry go on to serve in other roles during their conscription period.

In 2206, Emperor Krogan Lorkali reformed the Imperial Military ranking structure. This created distinct Officer and Other Ranks pathways for personnel and makes Krogan forces far more efficient than an odd NCO to Officer path previously implemented, along with rectifying a lack of enlisted level ranks. The current Imperial Armed Forces setup is far more effective than the initial setup.

Structure


Each Empire-loyal is allowed to retain its own fighting force. The training varies depending on the clan, as does equipment; some clan forces may put up a strong showing against Imperial Troopers with a possibility of victory, with others no better off than as a defensively-oriented militia force. The Empire asks of each clan 10% of their warriors for conscription into the Imperial Army. Clans tend to send their best, wanting to make a good showing. During training to become an Imperial Trooper, all conscripts first learn that they are brothers in arms, that their clan's name or prestige means little. They will obey the orders of their superiors or they will soon learn a very harsh lesson in reality. Those unable to adapt are usually forced into other duties fit for an undesirable, where those who learn and adapt go on to fill various combat roles, with top scorers in infantry. Those with keen intellect are usually slated for other roles which make the most use of their mind.

Asari who immigrate to the Empire as full citizens have a similar demand of them under Krogan conscription laws, given they live as long as Krogan can feasibly if not stupid. While they may have a service mandate, they do not have to serve it in the military, but can serve in any government or civil service role as well. Those who serve in the military tend to be careerists, Army and Navy alike, preferring to join for a commitment, rather than a short time. If aliens earn full citizenship within one of the Clans, or even the Empire at large, they are exempt from mandatory service, but may volunteer to serve.

The building block of the soldier forms the basis, from here, it is up the ladder all the way to the highest echelon, with the Emperor as Commander in Chief of the Krogan Armed Forces. The effort to coordinate the entire military in its day to day operations, as well as to advise the Emperor on military matters is the responsibility of the Joint Chief of Staff. The Krogan exist in a quasi-military controlled state of their Armed Forces, with the Emperor able to issue orders. While the Joint Chief does have the nominal head of the Minister of War, in practice, he answers directly to the Emperor, giving him a direct line to the Armed Forces that sidesteps the civilian appointee. Even if a lower rank than the Imperial Warmaster and Imperial Navalmaster, the Joint Chief is superior in command and will be paid proper respect.

The current Joint Chief of Staff is Warmaster Nakmor Khuyas.



Philosophy


In line with Emperor Blite's own words, as well as Imperial Warmaster Xarak's wise counsel, the Krogan Imperial Military, both Army and Navy alike, follow similar guidelines. Unlike generations gone by, this is not a gathering of the largest, strongest, most veteran of warriors to throw at the nearest obstacle. Blite, Xarak, and the High Overlords have used life lessons, experience, and research from across the galaxy to construct a training regime that would harness the raw fury that many knew the krogan for in combat, and turn it into a controllable, malleable force capable of acting as any professional army would.

Strength without order is chaos.
Strength without direction is squandered potential.
Strength without discipline is weakness.

Structure, order, and discipline has sharpened the dulled edge of a blade that was once krogan into something razor-honed and swift to strike with extreme precision and lethality. From reckless, brutal warriors to a well-trained, well-disciplined professional, the transformation of the species' military might into a vision of tomorrow has been something sorely needed and paramount to future success.

The Empire's primary military force is limited by the War Department's annual budget. Under Emperor Lorkali, the emphasis of the Krogan military has changed greatly; there is no disputing Krogan might on the ground, their technology is strong, their infantry strong; in fact, even the mighty Hierarchy Legions fighting the Imperial Army would lead to a gruesome slaughter in a ground campaign. Lorkali has recognized this, knowing no Council force would willingly send their forces into a grinder, and has shifted emphasis to the Imperial Navy. In the age of RKM weaponry, this emphasis has paid off, with the Krogan developing a unique doctrine to combat the most recent threat, and something that plays to their natural and national advantages. Only time can tell which untested new doctrine would be proven right and if the Krogan Empire may have invested much into a Navy only to see it proven naught but straw.



Training and Tactics


Imperial Hand-to-Hand/Melee Combat Training


-Para: A form of martial art developed by the Krogan, and often only Krogan were capable of performing it. While Krogan were more often known for their exertion of brute force, Para was surprisingly widely known, due to the natural violence of Krogan society and rites. Together with the natural strength of a giant reptile, it made a fearsome weapon. The martial art focused on using the immense upper body strength of the species, with an emphasis on punches, jabs and throws, and usually only a few strikes at a time. Also surprising were the non-lethal options available to the Krogan using the art, practitioners capable of disabling an opponent with a single blow.

- Living Ram: The style focuses on strong upper-body strikes powered by both muscle and body movement, as well as powerful knee strikes and stomping kicks. Defense relies primarily on avoiding or withstanding attacks.

The beginnings of Living Ram training are characterized by long, deep stances designed to develop strength in the martial artist's legs. Then, repetitive basic punches and kicks are added to improve muscle memory and develop upper-body strength and overall flexibility. Living Ram teachings were famous for their day-long training sessions where students would stay in a prescribed stance for upwards of ten hours, constantly punching and kicking. As training progressed, students were required to learn how to take blows from each other – as the student climbed in prowess, the blows would become more forceful.

Living Ram is characterized by hard upper-body strikes and stomping kicks. The attacks are designed to penetrate an opponent's guard through the sheer level of damage inflicted.

The art's name was taken from a story of the first Master, Jar Dol, who reputedly smashed open a meter-thick wooden gate with one blow of his fist. Because of the nature of the strikes, Living Ram practitioners tend to avoid secondary blocks, and train themselves to dodge or simply absorb attacks.

Lower-level students tend to dodge more, while more experienced fighters could shrug off most strikes. Masters of Living Ram were legendary for their resilience, able to brush away even the fiercest of unarmed blows.

-Rolling Boulder: A more recent addition to the training regime, one instructed and masterminded by Imperial Warmaster Ravanor Xarak. While Rolling Boulder is not by any means an original martial art, is is more or less his impressive experience and wisdom rolled into a complete package. After several centuries of traveling the galaxy as a mercenary, coming across all manner of foes of a wide variety of shapes and sizes, Xarak quickly saw the effectiveness of various techniques from different races. Utilizing this observation and curiosity, he went on to learn as much as possible about them, especially on the rare occasions he was contracted to work with others in a group.

Essentially, Rolling Boulder is as "un-krogan" of a fighting style in hand-to-hand and melee combat as one can think. It utilizes not the krogan raw strength and brute force of their size, but the opponent's own inertia against them. A more defensive, reactive style than offensive, it is meant to preserve as much energy as possible while incapacitating or killing the opponent with as little effort necessary. Brutally efficient and further enforced by a krogan practitioner, the style focuses upon parries, counters, quick strikes, sweeps, submissions. Targets are usually opponent joints, bones, and other critical locations meant to break, shatter, or dislocate to neutralize the threat.

Xarak's goal is to turn an Imperial Trooper with a utility blade into a force to be reckoned with, proving that the krogan are just as potentially lethal without a firearm, but a simple blade or even their bare hands. It is surprising to see a sense of fluidity about their movements with Rolling Boulder.

-Armed Melee: There are a number of Krogan martial arts that revolve around the use of weapons, not unlike Asari and Turian traditionalists. Krogan are known more for use of blunt weapons, warhammers and maces for instance, making use of a brutal weapon with a wider surface area to strike a target and bring the most use out of their natural upper body strength. There are also those who prefer the use of bladed weapons from longswords to battleaxes. These arts are meant for one real purpose, and that's to make sure the adversary doesn't get off the ground again. A Krogan even using the pommel of a sword to deliver a knockout strike has a possibility of fracturing a skull, defeating the idea of a non-lethal take down.

Ground Tactics


The Krogan are a strong military force on the ground. The discipline of Imperial Troopers, the implementation of combined arms tactics, learning from the past and using tactics other than swarm warfare have turned the Imperial Army into a force to be reckoned with. Some military analysts have found that it would take an entire Alliance tank squadron of 4-6 tanks to defeat a single Krogan MBT. The Imperial Army is, at this point, considered to be an equal to Citadel forces thanks to emphasis paid on the Imperial Army in the opening years of the Empire. While emphasis has shifted to the Navy, the Army still continues to train and use artillery, armor, naval and army air support, and infantry in deadly concert with each other. Anyone expecting an easy foe should think twice.

Naval Tactics


Krogan naval tactics are incredibly aggressive. These tactics have been developed by Imperial Navalmaster Mirah Zhenya before the advent of RKMs with the hope of breaking the stagnation of naval combat by forcing a more dynamic route. Her whole premise was to render the main weapon of an enemy's warship, the spinal gun, absolutely useless. The goal is to close to within 6 kilometers of the enemy (or closer even) in a full assault and then utilize the many cannons typically used for flak barriers to tear an enemy ship to death. Death by a thousand cuts rather than large blows. The Krogan also contributed, of their own design, saturation attacks with missiles from their cruisers. It's a highly aggressive naval tactic for a highly aggressive people, bringing all ships into combat than leave any sitting as targets for their enemy.

Imperial Army


Imperial Troopers are krogan warfare perfected, designed to be as ruthless, methodical, and efficient as possible, utilizing training and tactics rather than numbers due to the Genophage and controlled breeding after it was cured. There is no room for weakness in the Imperial Army, each Trooper must rely upon one another in full and entrust them with their lives. Imperial Troopers are conscripted for service annually from all Empire-loyal clans, taking 10% of their best warriors yearly to supplement them into the training regime, turning the best into the elite. Enduring the hardships of hand-to-hand combat training, melee combat training with all manner of weapons and tools, firearms proficiency, squad tactics, survival exercises, and all manner of other tests, over the course of 2 months, those that reach the end of the program with a sufficient passing score by overseeing Battlemasters acting as instructors graduate to join the ranks of the Imperial Army.

Imperial Navy


The Imperial Navy was first conceived as a support force with a poorly defined mission, showing the Krogan's lack of understanding of naval warfare. Under the guidance of Imperial Navalmaster Mirah Zhenya, the Krogan Navy has evolved from a force of no direction aside from causing damage to land on a planet with great haste to a force to be reckoned with. The Imperial Navy developed into a mainly defensive force, but has some offensive capabilities with limited power projection potential in the ability to land a single field army in an invasion at any one time. They are only limited by what the Hierarchy would permit, and Zhenya has very well made her plans known to them. They are not in conflict, but rather in sync.

Being a respectable size nation for Krogan and still expanding internally within areas they control, the Navy has the means to afford a suitable navy to patrol its many realms. New ships have been built in accordance with what Zhenya had envisioned as her “gray navy doctrine” prior to her becoming part of the Krogan Empire. This doctrine is based on the principle that the best defense is a good offense. Heavily reliant on aggressive ship movements to close the distance and the use of fighters for defense and superiority. This was later coupled with Krogan missile cruisers for saturation attacks. Krogan frigates remain in use as dropships to land troops onto a planet for a mass deployment before departing, keeping out of AAA range as they do so.

The Battle of Akuze and the Systems Alliance Civil War changed everything with the introduction of a new technology: FTL missiles. While this provides greater usefulness for Zhenya’s tactics in this new era of naval combat, and finally breaks the stagnation of ranged combat, it has thrown her plans into some tumult in reconfiguring the Navy’s size, maximum allowed per ship class, and develop yet more ships of other types to compensate for the new threat.

Even with some tumult in recent years, this is an innovator’s market in the system right now, and business is booming. The Krogan Imperial Navy is now a fine force suitable for a Grade A government, with Asari naval competency training Krogan in the art of naval warfare and refining those who already have experience. Pirates, raiders, slavers, and Independents should reconsider their life choices if they choose to fight the Imperial Navy.

In 07-2212, after departure from the Citadel, the Krogan Empire ceased using the KIN prefix on all vessels. Registration of vessels is still kept up to date thanks to close relations with the Hierarchy to allow for identification of Imperial Navy vessels based off the registry. Aside from previous Citadel obligations, the Krogan don't see a need for vessel prefixes. Some exterior writers continue to use KIN when referring to Imperial Navy specific vessels, however.

Economy


Prior to 2205 with Emperor Lorkali's ascension to the throne, the Krogan Empire operated a fully public sector economy, with all commercial ventures in the hands of either the Emperor himself or the various Clans. Upon ascension, Lorkali demolished the standing rule, decentralizing the economy and allowing the rise of private enterprise. This change has allowed a capitalist Krogan economy to rise from black markets that previously existed under Blite's system. Within 5 years, the Imperial economy has emerged from a spiraling debt that would have led to a massive breakdown in the economy to a self-sustaining and budding economy with private ventures taking over where government owned ventures once existed.

Empire General Assembly, once operating under complete government control, has been allowed to act autonomously under government oversight. While Kolvant Garoth is still proud to produce weapons and machines of war, EGA now churns out civilian vehicles, household appliances, and other civilian goods that might be needed within the Empire. Investments made by EGA has helped to boost income, in which they are now starting to see the first returns. Private enterprises have recently been allowed to lease EGA's orbital shipyards, especially with recent orders to expand operations, to build their own vessels.

History

Much of the Empire's history leading to its inception is tied up with that of Woade Blite. Blite laid much of the ground work for Krogan advancement, this cannot go without saying. The Rebellions required a new course to take...but Blite's would unfortunately doom Krogan for yet another round of the same destruction as last time. In the end, it was expansionism, the very thing he saw as needed to abate Krogan agression, that did him in. His initial commitment and withdrawal from the Systems Alliance Civil War resulted in Krogan who initially supported him turning against him, some calling for his abdication, others for his head. In 2205, Blite stepped down from the throne and retired.

In his place rose Battlecaptain Urdnot Lorkali. Logical, philosophical, a scholarly warrior taking a strong pulse of the Krogan on domestic policy, though a novice on the international stage. It's now up to this Battlecaptain turned Emperor to lead the Krogan to their future, one that shouldn't result in another Rebellions and their extermination.

Timeline

Approx 4000 years prior to 2183: The Krogan, technologically advanced and having overcome the rigors of living on Tuchanka with its hostile wildlife, began fighting each other. The Krogan began fighting each other. A formerly culturally rich people with architectural and artistic achievements of their own fought over small tracts of land because of an exploding population. Krogan destroy their entire civilization in nuclear hellfire, turning Tuchanka into a vast wasteland. Those with Blood Rage, a mental illness as far as Krogan are concerned, become some of the strongest survivors in this post-apocalyptic world.

1 - 80: The Salarians open an unopened relay and discover the Rachni. This starts the chain of events that leads to the uplifting of the Krogan as the Citadel Council's military force in fighting the Rachni, believing the bugs are hostile beyond redemption. The Krogan are given new technology and worlds in exchange for foot soldiers. The year 80 is when Krogan begin arriving in force and begin taking the fight to the Rachni.

81 - 300: The Krogan brutally fight the Rachni, their ability to reproduce with great haste gives them the edge in a fight against the insect race. Krogan fight the Rachni all the way back to Suen and destroy their hives. This leads to the Citadel government declaring the Rachni extinct by 300 and leaving a listening post for possible survivors. The Krogan are hailed as heroes and a statue on the Presidium is raised in honor of their accomplishments.

301 - 700: Off Tuchanka, Krogan, failing to learn from their past, begin reproducing exponentially, claiming other worlds by force. They even begin taking worlds held by other Council members, each time claiming they needed just "one more world" for living space. One such world is Garvug, a formerly lush planet which the Krogan have ecologically ruined thanks to their poor practices in planetary upkeep. The Krogan then began inhabiting the Asari world of Lusia; the Council refused to hand control of Lusia over to the Krogan and ordered them off the planet. Overlord Kredak, Krogan ambassador to the Council, dared the Council to come take their planets back by force and stormed out of the Council Chambers. This act is considered the start of The Krogan Rebellions.

701 - ~1000s: When the Rebellions ended is a matter of debate, some say as late as the 1000s, some say at ~780 when Parthian forces nuked the Krogan world of Canrum. The Rebellions were the first official action of the Spectres, easily able to replenish their numbers against Asari and Salarian forces. It took the newcomer to the spacefaring community, the Turians, to prove a brutal match. Krogan used total war, just as the Turians did, so when Turians faced asteroid strikes against several of their colonies rendering them uninhabitable, the Hierarchy lashed back in kind. The resulting action led to the development of the Genophage and the distribution in 710, the crippling virus killing their reproductive capability. Unable to replenish their numbers, Krogan increasingly quit fighting in the face of an unsustainable war, leading many to surrender by the mid 700s. Rogue warlords continued to fight in the meantime and were brutally put down by Legion forces, others taking their bands and leaving to the frontiers and becoming pirates. In any event, the war was over, the Krogan lost, and were in a horrific post-war position.

~770s - 2203: With the Genophage, and unable to replenish their numbers as before, the Krogan begin their decline. As time goes on, many Krogan believe that without a cure or some change, it will mean the extinction of their people by the end of the 23rd century. Blood Rage has become so pervasive in Krogan society that those without it start becoming rare, but may still come about. This mental illness sees Krogan more focused on fighting each other and militaristic pursuits rather than focusing on reversing the damage. Intellectuals are sneered at and mocked by society. Even the remaining small numbers of Krogan engaged in sciences and philosophy see their own people as a lost cause, but not everyone gives up.

Imperial Armed Forces

Training and Tactics


Imperial Hand-to-Hand/Melee Combat Training


-Para: A form of martial art developed by the Krogan, and often only Krogan were capable of performing it. While Krogan were more often known for their exertion of brute force, Para was surprisingly widely known, due to the natural violence of Krogan society and rites. Together with the natural strength of a giant reptile, it made a fearsome weapon. The martial art focused on using the immense upper body strength of the species, with an emphasis on punches, jabs and throws, and usually only a few strikes at a time. Also surprising were the non-lethal options available to the Krogan using the art, practitioners capable of disabling an opponent with a single blow.

- Living Ram: The style focuses on strong upper-body strikes powered by both muscle and body movement, as well as powerful knee strikes and stomping kicks. Defense relies primarily on avoiding or withstanding attacks.

The beginnings of Living Ram training are characterized by long, deep stances designed to develop strength in the martial artist's legs. Then, repetitive basic punches and kicks are added to improve muscle memory and develop upper-body strength and overall flexibility. Living Ram teachings were famous for their day-long training sessions where students would stay in a prescribed stance for upwards of ten hours, constantly punching and kicking. As training progressed, students were required to learn how to take blows from each other – as the student climbed in prowess, the blows would become more forceful.

Living Ram is characterized by hard upper-body strikes and stomping kicks. The attacks are designed to penetrate an opponent's guard through the sheer level of damage inflicted.

The art's name was taken from a story of the first Master, Jar Dol, who reputedly smashed open a meter-thick wooden gate with one blow of his fist. Because of the nature of the strikes, Living Ram practitioners tend to avoid secondary blocks, and train themselves to dodge or simply absorb attacks.

Lower-level students tend to dodge more, while more experienced fighters could shrug off most strikes. Masters of Living Ram were legendary for their resilience, able to brush away even the fiercest of unarmed blows.

-Rolling Boulder: A more recent addition to the training regime, one instructed and masterminded by Imperial Warmaster Ravanor Xarak. While Rolling Boulder is not by any means an original martial art, is is more or less his impressive experience and wisdom rolled into a complete package. After several centuries of traveling the galaxy as a mercenary, coming across all manner of foes of a wide variety of shapes and sizes, Xarak quickly saw the effectiveness of various techniques from different races. Utilizing this observation and curiosity, he went on to learn as much as possible about them, especially on the rare occasions he was contracted to work with others in a group.

Essentially, Rolling Boulder is as "un-krogan" of a fighting style in hand-to-hand and melee combat as one can think. It utilizes not the krogan raw strength and brute force of their size, but the opponent's own inertia against them. A more defensive, reactive style than offensive, it is meant to preserve as much energy as possible while incapacitating or killing the opponent with as little effort necessary. Brutally efficient and further enforced by a krogan practitioner, the style focuses upon parries, counters, quick strikes, sweeps, submissions. Targets are usually opponent joints, bones, and other critical locations meant to break, shatter, or dislocate to neutralize the threat.

Xarak's goal is to turn an Imperial Trooper with a utility blade into a force to be reckoned with, proving that the krogan are just as potentially lethal without a firearm, but a simple blade or even their bare hands. It is surprising to see a sense of fluidity about their movements with Rolling Boulder.

-Armed Melee: There are a number of Krogan martial arts that revolve around the use of weapons, not unlike Asari and Turian traditionalists. Krogan are known more for use of blunt weapons, warhammers and maces for instance, making use of a brutal weapon with a wider surface area to strike a target and bring the most use out of their natural upper body strength. There are also those who prefer the use of bladed weapons from longswords to battleaxes. These arts are meant for one real purpose, and that's to make sure the adversary doesn't get off the ground again. A Krogan even using the pommel of a sword to deliver a knockout strike has a possibility of fracturing a skull, defeating the idea of a non-lethal take down.

Ground Tactics


The Krogan are a strong military force on the ground. The discipline of Imperial Troopers, the implementation of combined arms tactics, learning from the past and using tactics other than swarm warfare have turned the Imperial Army into a force to be reckoned with. Some military analysts have found that it would take an entire Alliance tank squadron of 4-6 tanks to defeat a single Krogan MBT. The Imperial Army is, at this point, considered to be an equal to Citadel forces thanks to emphasis paid on the Imperial Army in the opening years of the Empire. While emphasis has shifted to the Navy, the Army still continues to train and use artillery, armor, naval and army air support, and infantry in deadly concert with each other. Naval Tactics


Krogan naval tactics are incredibly aggressive. These tactics have been developed by Imperial Navalmaster Mirah Zhenya before the advent of RKMs with the hope of breaking the stagnation of naval combat by forcing a more dynamic route. Her whole premise was to render the main weapon of an enemy's warship, the spinal gun, absolutely useless. The goal is to close to within 6 kilometers of the enemy (or closer even) in a full assault and then utilize the many cannons typically used for flak barriers to tear an enemy ship to death. Death by a thousand cuts rather than large blows. The Krogan also contributed, of their own design, saturation attacks with missiles from their cruisers. It's a highly aggressive naval tactic for a highly aggressive people, bringing all ships into combat than leave any sitting as targets for their enemy.

Imperial Army


Imperial Troopers are krogan warfare perfected, designed to be as ruthless, methodical, and efficient as possible, utilizing training and tactics rather than numbers due to the Genophage and controlled breeding after it was cured. There is no room for weakness in the Imperial Army, each Trooper must rely upon one another in full and entrust them with their lives. What they lack in numbers, they make up with superior training and an unbreakable will of determination and sense of duty in loyal service to the advancement of the krogan once more.

Imperial Troopers are conscripted for service annually from all Empire-loyal clans, taking 10% of their best warriors yearly to supplement them into the training regime, turning the best into the elite. Enduring the hardships of hand-to-hand combat training, melee combat training with all manner of weapons and tools, firearms proficiency, squad tactics, survival exercises, and all manner of other tests, over the course of 2 months, those that reach the end of the program with a sufficient passing score by overseeing Battlemasters acting as instructors graduate to join the ranks of the Imperial Army.

Those that fail to make the cut of Imperial Troopers, but managed to survive the training are allowed to attempt as many times necessary. If they desire not to, they are placed in lesser/supportive roles, such as the Imperial Armored Corps. For a krogan, while being at the helm of a 300 ton rolling death machine is a dream come true, they have several inches of armor plating and barriers between them and the action, not exactly thrilling or respectable by other krogan of what they do. All desire to be Imperial Troopers, not all are capable, it is reality.

Non-Krogan forces, small in number, are organized into rear echelon units. Alien infantry units are extremely uncommon, and are usually platoons attached to second line Krogan units at best. Usually, aliens serve in non-combat duties but can still fight if the enemy comes to them. Though, some officers from Senawuk's Utopiate Eternal Guard have come to the Empire to assist Krogan officers in training their forces in the finer arts of Asari warfare from scouting missions to laying ambushes. The expertise of aliens brought to the table have been welcomed by higher ranking Krogan echelons to turn the Imperial Army into a well rounded force rather than the main Krogan focus on blunt force and overwhelming firepower.

Imperial Navy


The Imperial Navy was first conceived as a support force with a poorly defined mission, showing the Krogan's lack of understanding of naval warfare. Under the guidance of Imperial Navalmaster Mirah Zhenya, the Krogan Navy has evolved from a force of no direction aside from causing damage to land on a planet with great haste to a force to be reckoned with. The Imperial Navy developed into a mainly defensive force, but has some offensive capabilities with limited power projection potential in the ability to land a single field army in an invasion at any one time. They are only limited by what the Hierarchy would permit, and Zhenya has very well made her plans known to them. They are not in conflict, but rather in sync.

Being a respectable size nation for Krogan and still expanding internally within areas they control, the Navy has the means to afford a suitable navy to patrol its many realms. New ships have been built in accordance with what Zhenya had envisioned as her “gray navy doctrine” prior to her becoming part of the Krogan Empire. This doctrine is based on the principle that the best defense is a good offense. Heavily reliant on aggressive ship movements to close the distance and the use of fighters for defense and superiority. This was later coupled with Krogan missile cruisers for saturation attacks. Krogan frigates remain in use as dropships to land troops onto a planet for a mass deployment before departing, keeping out of AAA range as they do so.

The Battle of Akuze and the Systems Alliance Civil War changed everything with the introduction of a new technology: FTL missiles. While this provides greater usefulness for Zhenya’s tactics in this new era of naval combat, and finally breaks the stagnation of ranged combat, it has thrown her plans into some tumult in reconfiguring the Navy’s size, maximum allowed per ship class, and develop yet more ships of other types to compensate for the new threat.

Even with some tumult in recent years, this is an innovator’s market in the system right now, and business is booming. The Krogan Imperial Navy is now a fine force suitable for a Grade A government, with Asari naval competency training Krogan in the art of naval warfare and refining those who already have experience. Pirates, raiders, slavers, and Independents should reconsider their life choices if they choose to fight the Imperial Navy.

Overall, the IIA is a brutally efficient force. They are the glue that holds the empire together, keeping the clans united under the law and maintaining relative peace to ensure infighting does not cause the krogan people to become weak due to instability. Determined to make a new name for themselves amongst the universe as a people ready to cooperate and contribute to the galaxy, the krogan strive for strength through peace and stability.

Economy


Prior to 2205 with Emperor Lorkali's ascension to the throne, the Krogan Empire operated a fully public sector economy, with all commercial ventures in the hands of either the Emperor himself or the various Clans. Upon ascension, Lorkali demolished the standing rule, decentralizing the economy and allowing the rise of private enterprise. This change has allowed a capitalist Krogan economy to rise from black markets that previously existed under Blite's system. Within 5 years, the Imperial economy has emerged from a spiraling debt that would have led to a massive breakdown in the economy to a self-sustaining and budding economy with private ventures taking over where government owned ventures once existed.

Empire General Assembly, once operating under complete government control, has been allowed to act autonomously under government oversight. While Kolvant Garoth is still proud to produce weapons and machines of war, EGA now churns out civilian vehicles, household appliances, and other civilian goods that might be needed within the Empire. Investments made by EGA has helped to boost income, in which they are now starting to see the first returns. Private enterprises have recently been allowed to lease EGA's orbital shipyards, especially with recent orders to expand operations, to build their own vessels.
