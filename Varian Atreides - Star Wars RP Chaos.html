STRENGTHS AND WEAKNESSES

  • + NATURAL CONDUIT
     By design, Hadrian has the innate ability to allow the flow of the natural, living Force to go through him and gather like a capacitor. As such, he is capable of temporarily harnessing that raw excess of the universe and bending it to his will. He can also tap into the Cosmic Force to enhance the capabilities his already prodigious Sense and Telekinetic abilities.
     
  • + WARRIOR SCHOLAR
     Defined by his intellect, strength of will, and extensive knowledge across multiple fields ranging from military tactics and engineering to the arcane powers of the Force and alchemy, he is still a highly capable combatant with both the Lightsaber and the Force. In addition, due to time spent learning under an old Mandalorian Elder, he has picked up various skills with both blasters and other standard ranged weaponry as well as blacksmithing and maintaining his own equipment.
     
  • - TECHNOLATROPHOBIA
    Even after all this time, Varian is still nervous around droid medical personnel.
  • - FOCUSED
    No matter what relation you have with Varian, he takes his work seriously. Including battle. So any time that he will be in battle, he won't refer to you as the nicknames he may call you by, or by other non formal terms. He takes battle, and "work" in general very serious.

 

APPEARANCE

 With a slimmer athletic build, Asher's form is not typically imposing when met face-to-face. He has kept most of his youth, though the traumas of life have hardened his face and brought a weight to his walk. With clothes on, he appears to be a normal human, with no markings or scars, but if you remove his clothing then he is littered with hair-line scars; the result of his near-death at Naboo.

 

PERSONALITY

 Work and play don't mix, but different work calls for different minds. When at war, Valentine is a nucleus for death and destruction, leaving little in his wake save his allies. In other environments, however, Asher can be very curious and inquisitive. On a day-to-day basis, there is an air of mischief about him as he walks on the balls of his feet and watches little movements like a predator curiously watches its prey. He is capable of laughter, humour, and play, but his reputation on a battlefield is what most would expect from him.

ABILITY SKILLS

  • Acrobatics: Acrobatics check covers your attempt to stay on your feet in a tricky situation, such as when you're trying to run across a sheet of ice, balance on a tightrope, or stay upright on a rocking ship's deck. There might also be Acrobatics checks to see if you can perform acrobatic stunts, including dives, rolls, somersaults, and flips.
  • Animal Handling: When there is any question whether you can calm down a domesticated animal, keep a mount from getting spooked, or intuit an animal’s intentions, an Animal Handling check is made. There might also be Animal Handling check to control your mount when you attempt a risky maneuver.
  • Arcana: Arcana check measures your ability to recall lore about spells, magic items, eldritch symbols, magical traditions, the planes of existence, and the inhabitants of those planes.
  • Athletics: Athletics check covers difficult situations you encounter while climbing, jumping, or swimming.
  • Deception: Deception check determines whether you can convincingly hide the truth, either verbally or through your actions. This deception can encompass everything from misleading others through ambiguity to telling outright lies. Typical situations include trying to fast-talk a guard, con a merchant, earn money through gambling, pass yourself off in a disguise, dull someone's suspicions with false assurances, or maintain a straight face while telling a blatant lie.
  • History: History check measures your ability to recall lore about historical events, legendary people, ancient kingdoms, past disputes, recent wars, and lost civilizations.
  • Insight: Insight check decides whether you can determine the true intentions of a creature, such as when searching out a lie or predicting someone’s next move. Doing so involves gleaning clues from body language, speech habits, and changes in mannerisms.
  • Intimidation: When you attempt to influence someone through overt threats, hostile actions, and physical violence, an Intimidation check is made. Examples include trying to pry information out of a prisoner, convincing street thugs to back down from a confrontation, or using the edge of a broken bottle to convince a sneering vizier to reconsider a decision.
  • Investigation: When you look around for clues and make deductions based on those clues, you make an Investigation check. You might deduce the location of a hidden object, discern from the appearance of a wound what kind of weapon dealt it, or determine the weakest point in a tunnel that could cause it to collapse. Poring through ancient scrolls in search of a hidden fragment of knowledge might also call for an Investigation check.
  • Medicine: Medicine check lets you try to stabilize a dying companion or diagnose an illness.
  • Nature: Nature check measures your ability to recall lore about terrain, plants and animals, the weather, and natural cycles.
  • Perception: Perception check lets you spot, hear, or otherwise detect the presence of something. It measures your general awareness of your surroundings and the keenness of your senses. For example, you might try to hear a conversation through a closed door, eavesdrop under an open window, or hear monsters moving stealthily in the forest. Or you might try to spot things that are obscured or easy to miss, whether they are orcs lying in ambush on a road, thugs hiding in the shadows of an alley, or candlelight under a closed secret door.
  • Performance: Performance check determines how well you can delight an audience with music, dance, acting, storytelling, or some other form of entertainment.
  • Persuasion: When you attempt to influence someone or a group of people with tact, social graces, or good nature, a Persuasion check is made. Typically, you use persuasion when acting in good faith, to foster friendships, make cordial requests, or exhibit proper etiquette. Examples of persuading others include convincing a chamberlain to let your party see the king, negotiating peace between warring tribes, or inspiring a crowd of townsfolk.
  • Religion: Religion check measures your ability to recall lore about deities, rites and prayers, religious hierarchies, holy symbols, and the practices of secret cults.
  • Sleight of Hand: Whenever you attempt an act of legerdemain or manual trickery, such as planting something on someone else or concealing an object on your person, make a Sleight of Hand check. There might also be Sleight of Hand checks to determine whether you can lift a coin purse off another person or slip something out of another person's pocket.
  • Stealth: Stealth checks when you attempt to conceal yourself from enemies, slink past guards, slip away without being noticed, or sneak up on someone without being seen or heard.
  • Survival: Survival check to follow tracks, hunt wild game, guide your group through frozen wastelands, identify signs that owlbears live nearby, predict the weather, or avoid quicksand and other natural hazards.
