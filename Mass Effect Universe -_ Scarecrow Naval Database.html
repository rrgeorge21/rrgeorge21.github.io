Filler Img from Star Citizen

Created by the Scarecrows in 2205 shortly after the capturing of the Grail shipyards, they quickly realized with their growths that they would need to expand their naval efforts. The biggest being the ability to move large quantities of troops and gear from world to world for their clients. To these ends the crows took old Galactic industry designs from the data base at the shipyards, using them as a base line they built a well armored transport capable of moving their troops from point B to point A without loses. Near the end of 2205 the first Orru’s would see flight in the crow navies, capable of transporting thousands of troops, gear and vehicles for ground campaigns they prove to be the back bone of the crow navy.When compared to other transports and freighters of their class the ships are much more armored and defended permitting them to take a few hits from opposing vessels and often shrug off engagements with lighter craft. Though like many other crow vessels the freighters armor has lead to a lose of mobility ensuring if swarmed by enough ships it will still perish in battle. This has led the crows to never deploy the ships beyond the safety of their escorts or beyond safe trade lanes. Well the ship should be able to push through during naval engagements and land troops to assist friendly forces the crows have yet to test them in such conditions and fear the lose of their new vessels.

With a squarish shape its silhouette is easy to spot on the battlefield its armor being the biggest saving grace, it also features a large commpement of GUARDIAN clusters and Light MAAC turrets that permit it to screen interceptors and bombers alongside other vessels with ease. Though beyond this it has little to no capability to handle opposing warships having to rely heavily on friendly vessels to keep the lanes clear of enemy warships that might seek to stop the crows from reinforcing their ground positions. Often seen within the Terminus in small patrols and ferrying supplies between several Crow bases and facilities it has proven to be a effective transport and ship of the crows military development into the naval direction.

Due to the massive growth of Scarecrow forces the crow’s infrastructure to refit and build new Orru’s has easily fallen behind given they’re not purposed to build ships as a company, forcing them to rely on Selachi Industries to handle their new production go 2207 and on ward instead focusing on the refitting and repair of all ready produced ships being their infrastructures limit. Newer built ones are nicknamed variant B’s due to the fact they also got a minor upgrade in armoring plating and defense grid. Well crows have contemplated upgrading the older Variant A’s to meet B standards the actual statistic difference has lead to it simply not being economical, with the B’s being a very slight upgrade.Though it still has garnered a nickname by Crow ground forces as a flying brick due to its squarish shape and even nicknamed the Crow's Coffin given the troops inability to actually do anything aboard during naval combat to protect their lives.

Sending out agents to locate possible alternates and conducting talks with Terminus ship producers including Selachi. Testing their capabilities against pirates in preparation of joining the war alongside the Attican cause. Well the ship is the lightest vessel the Crow’s make use of it still features the heavy plating common with all their craft ensuring it can take a punch or two of ships of the same class or one step higher. But this cost it the fast maneuverability that is a norm for other corvettes, relying more on being a fighter screen for large ships and destroying ships of the same class with its heavy armament.

When hunting in packs even larger classed ships need to be mindful of the Typicus since it can still do damage and take it in even strides. Use during pirate hunting operations have showed such results with many Typicus plates all ready nicked from close calls with enemy rounds, well not as equally armed when compared to the Crows the pirates still mounted resistance and the Typicus showed itself to be a good practical ship for the Crows heavy armor doctrine.
