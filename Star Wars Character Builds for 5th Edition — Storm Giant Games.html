Star Wars Character Builds for 5th Edition

In last week’s article, How To Build Disney Characters in 5e, we talked a little bit about how many players like to take their inspiration for their character from their favorite fiction franchises. Whether you want to replicate playing as a character you love in your DND game, you relate to a character on an emotional level or you just want to build them and use their stats on your character sheet - it’s a very valid way to build a character and I don’t think it’s discussed enough. Very rarely do new players who are unfamiliar with RPGs have the ability to flex their creative muscles both from a story perspective or an optimization perspective to create a truly unique Dungeons & Dragons character. At the end of the day, the character you build is just stats on your character sheet and they really come to life with your roleplaying. You may choose to play them similarly to the character you modeled them off of or you may end up playing them completely differently. Regardless, I’m a huge advocate of building characters in this way and I think almost everyone at my table has done it at least once.

So, as I was writing last weeks article I began to realize that my list of Disney characters wasn’t quite complete. Technically, Star Wars characters are also Disney characters now. Actually, Marvel characters are technically Disney characters too. I’ll be building those in the future too but for now, if you’re a dungeon master, take a look at the 5e Thanos Stats we did a while back.

Dungeons and Dragons goes hand in hand with Star Wars for those who are fans of both. Personally, I can easily watch the movies and break down the action and events into 5e terms I understand. When a combat breaks out, the cinematic perspective begins to morph into an action economy in my head. The politics and side quests that are prevalent in every Star Wars movie are the hallmark of an extremely well planned D&D campaign. And the character archetypes that exist in Star Wars are often still there in D&D - such as the comparison between Sith and liches. So naturally, people who think in a similar way as I do would also imagine whether or not their favorite Star Wars characters can be recreated through the framework of a 5e character sheet.

I’m here to tell you that the answer is a resounding yes. It doesn’t matter where or when or even how you want to play them - it can absolutely be done. There are a lot of ways your DM can create a campaign that lets your table play Star Wars for 5th Edition. There’s even a Star Wars campaign setting for 5th Edition that’s known as SW5E. Even if your DM doesn’t give a damn about Star Wars and just wants to play a vanilla Forgotten Realms campaign, you can still translate these characters. Maybe just avoid calling them by the same names. If you’ve got a DM that’s at all willing to bring aspects of Spelljammer into your game, your Star Wars characters will be right at home in space - though Spelljammer is going to be a bit more campy than Hoth or Endor. Nonetheless, if you’re into Star Wars and you’re into DND, you’ve come to the right place.

I’ll try to be as setting and level agnostic as possible with these, as some D&D tables start at varying levels and with varying rulesets. Point buy vs rolling vs standard array can change the way you might design these, but I’ll use point buy, since it seems to be the most common.

So let’s get started.

 
 

Boba Fett

Bounty hunter characters can be found all over fiction, in anything from sci-fi to high fantasy. Perhaps none more iconic than the Mandalorian called Boba Fett. With his high flying jetpack and a veritable armory strapped to his armor at all times, Boba Fett could embody several D&D character classes. For one, Matt Mercer’s gunslinger fighter could actually pull off a Boba Fett build fairly well, though you would need quite a few magic items to get the feel right. An artificer could also work really well for a Mandalorian - except then you’re missing quite a bit of the hand-to-hand combat prowess. But I think the ranger class does a great job of pulling off a Boba Fett build.

Boba Fett

Ranger (Hunter)

Variant Human, Criminal, Neutral Evil

STR 10 DEX 18 CON 15 INT 11 WIS 12 CHA 10

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Acrobatics +7, Athletics +3, Perception +4, Survival +4

  • Feats: Crossbow Expert, Magic Initiate (Wizard). Take light, shocking grasp and burning hands to replicate some of the abilities of Boba Fett’s Mandalorian armor.

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: If your game has guns, pick up a rifle. If it doesn’t you can pretty much get what you need out of a crossbow.

  • Favored Enemy should be humanoids to really encapsulate the bounty hunter trait. Pick up whatever additional favored enemies you’d like later.

  • Fighting Style. Defense fighting style helps you get the heavily armored feel. Heavy armor doesn’t actually make sense for Boba in D&D due to the fact that his Dexterity is so high, but that’s okay.

  • Ranger Spells are where we really get the features of Boba Fetts armor and hunting skills to come out. Hunter’s mark is an obvious choice. Alarm, detect magic, fog cloud, jump, snare, cordon of arrows, darkvision and silence are all great choices. Many other ranger spells could fit if you want to switch it up as well.

Magic Items:

  • Boots of Levitation. My backpack’s got jets.

  • Helm of Comprehending Languages. Boba Fett pretty much has exactly this.

 
 

C3P-O

In Dungeons and Dragons terms, C3P-O isn’t a very effective character to play. He’s not much of a fighter, isn’t a medic/healer and he doesn’t have any “magical” powers. But he’s one of my favorite Star Wars characters so dammit I’m going to give it a try. Droids in general aren’t really great for player characters but they would make excellent companions. That’s what I suggest you do if you want to play C3P-O, build him with a droid companion named R2D2. Your character sheet is going to be stretched a little thin to build C3P-O in the way I propose but it’ll be fun as hell. My favorite combination off classes: The Bardificer.

C3P-O

Bard (College of Eloquence)/Artificer (Battle Smith)

Warforged, Sage, Neutral Good

STR 10 DEX 10 CON 14 INT 18 WIS 8 CHA 15

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +7, History +7, Insight +2, Medicine +2, Nature +7, Religion +7

  • Feats: Actor, Keen Mind, Linguist, Observant

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Not much equipment really needed here but any set of tools you can pick up would be good.

  • Steel Defender is your droid companion, R2D2.

  • The Right Tool For The Job lets you create any set of tools you may not already have. Bonus points if you flavor this as your R2 companion using its internal tools.

  • Universal Speech from college of eloquence is goddamn perfect for a druid fluent in over six million forms of communication.

  • Spells. Both the bard and artificer list get you some interesting and useful spells. From the bard spell list, pick up message, vicious mockery, comprehend languages, detect magic, identify, speak with animals, magic mouth, and see invisibility. From the artificer spell list you can get catapult, grease, aid and darkvision. Not the best spells from these lists, I know - but they’re fitting.

Magic Items:

  • Helm of Comprehending Languages. You’ll need this if you want there to be no doubt that you can translate any communication.

 
 

Chewbacca

Now this is a fun D&D build. Pretty straightforward, but I think it would be so much fun to play. If ever I was playing in a Star Wars 5e game that consisted of existing characters, Chewbacca would be my pick. I’ve seen homebrew Wookiee races but I think you can still get what you need from goliath or half orc. Thematically, minotaur is pretty close but the racial abilities don’t really fit Chewy so we’ll just make a super hairy goliath crossbow fighter. Bugbear is also a perfectly good substitute for goliath and is more thematic but I like the goliath racial features better for him. Either one is a good choice.

Chewbacca

Fighter (Battlemaster)

Goliath, Smuggler, Neutral Good

STR 16 DEX 16 CON 15 INT 10 WIS 10 CHA 10

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Athletics +6, Deception +3, Intimidation +3, Perception +3

  • Feats: Crossbow Expert, Sharpshooter, Tavern Brawler

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Crossbow.

  • Goliath Racial Traits get you a lot of excellent wookiee flavor from stone’s endurance, powerful build and mountain born.

  • Fighter lets you be a savage wookiee with a buttload of attacks and able to hold your own in melee. Though Chewy is strong, he’s primarily ranged with his bowcaster. Second wind as a bonus action must be accompanied by a growl.

  • Battlemaster Manuevers. Any will fit, though I chose rally, disarming attack and pushing attack.

Magic Items:

  • Any Magical Crossbow. Bonus points if the bolts are flavored as lasers.

 
 

Darth Maul

Sith lords make excellent NPCs but they make excellent PCs as well. A bit dark and edgy, if you can pull off the personality within your party or if you’re playing an all sith adventure, sith lord builds are an absolute blast in Dungeons and Dragons. Darth Maul is a particularly fun one but a bit hard to pull off his fighting style with his weapon. If you have a variant 5e rule about custom weapons, maybe a double bladed scimitar or something crazy would work but I believe that because of his fighting style, he would be pretty adept with a polearm. A kensei monk could be fitting but it doesn’t really get us the force powers we want. Therefore, I’m going to have to go with hexblade.

Darth Maul

Warlock (Hexblade)

Tiefling, Soldier, Chaotic Evil

STR 10 DEX 16 CON 14 INT 11 WIS 10 CHA 16

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +3, Athletics +3, Intimidation +6, Perception +3

  • Feats: Polearm Master, Sentinel

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: As discussed earlier, 5e rules don’t really have double ended weapons but you can get a polearm. Of course, anyone who carries a lightsaber would want a sunblade to truly represent a lightsaber.

  • Create Pact Weapon gets you a cool feeling of activating a lightsaber by making your weapon appear in your hand instantaneously.

  • Eldritch Invocations work better for sith than they do for Jedi knights, IMO. Flavor your eldritch blast as force powers. Repelling blast and grasp of hadar work great for force push and force pull, respectively. But the other invocations are much more sith than they are Jedi. Maddening hex, for example.

  • Warlock Spells. Make your eldritch blast invisible and pick up mage hand and prestidigitation to represent manipulating the force. Cause fear, hex, witch bolt, wrathful smite, crown of madness, earthbind, shadow blade, shatter, and fear are great spells for your sith “spellbook”.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. This is almost exactly a lightsaber. Hug your DM and try to get a double ended version for your Darth Maul adventure.

 
 

Darth Vader

Darth Vader has got to be one of the most heavily discussed and hotly debated 5e character builds of all time. He’s extremely difficult to make and represent, at least until you have a high level build. Some people will claim that eldritch knight can represent him well and it can at lower levels. But if you really want the best Vader build you can get, I wouldn’t try to tackle it until at least level 10. His combat prowess and force powers are pretty much untouched in Star Wars lore and that makes him difficult to build accurately. However, you can do it right with oath of conquest (which is pretty much made to be Darth Vader) and a few levels of hexblade. Paladin 7/Warlock 3 is where Vader’s character sheet really clicks. If you wanted to truly accurately represent him, he would be a very MAD character - but I think you can get what you need without going that route. His strength, intelligence and wisdom won’t be what they should but you can fake that a little through skill proficiencies.

Darth Vader

Paladin (Oath of Conquest)/Warlock (Hexblade)

Warforged, Soldier, Neutral Evil

STR 10 DEX 16 CON 16 INT 10 WIS 10 CHA 16

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +4, Athletics +4, Intimidation +6, Religion +4

  • Feats: Tough, Sentinel

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Vader’s only truly iconic piece of equipment is a lightsaber. If he was using a sword, he strikes me as a bit of a greatsword kind of guy so switch his dexterity and strength.

  • Aura of Conquest is absolutely awesome for Vader. Hell, I think I had the frightened condition when he came on screen in Rogue One.

  • Eldritch Invocations let you display some iconic Vader force powers. Repelling blast and grasp of hadar work great for force push and force pull, respectively.

  • Warlock Spells. As with Maul, make your eldritch blast invisible and pick up mage hand and prestidigitation to represent manipulating the force. Also take cause fear, hex, witch bolt, wrathful smite, crown of madness, earthbind, shadow blade, shatter, and fear.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. What you need to accurately portray a lightsaber.

 
 

Finn

Finn doesn’t display a whole lot of abilities in 5e terms, but that’s okay. He’s got the personality for a fantastic D&D character and we have the fighter and rogue classes to build the more “unskilled” characters. Finn gives me more of a rogue vibe but I wouldn’t argue at all with building him as a fighter. Additionally, you could make an argument that some of the subclass features from thief, mastermind or inquisitive fit him but I think he fits in with the party best as a scout rogue.

Finn

Rogue (Scout)

Human, Soldier, Neutral Good

STR 10 DEX 18 CON 14 INT 12 WIS 12 CHA 14

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Athletics +3, Insight +7, Nature +7, Persuasion +7, Stealth +7, Survival +7

  • Feats: Lucky

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Nothing really noteworthy here except maybe picking up a blaster. A hand crossbow will do just fine.

  • Skirmisher works really well for my mental vision of Finn, even though he hasn’t necessarily displayed that.

  • Survivalist seems like a good display of the skills he would have picked up as a stormtrooper.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. Well, he did have a light saber once!

 
 

Han Solo

There’s an old D&D joke that says that Han Solo is basically just a high charisma rogue with terrible dice rolls and a +10 to initiative (so he always shoots first). Only, I don’t think that’s really much of a joke at all. I think it’s pretty spot on. I’ve debated what subclass fits. I think swashbuckler kind of gets you there, though it’s built more to be a melee focused subclass so I find it a bit dubious. Mastermind and thief also are close, but I don’t see them as a perfect fit either. I’m going to go with a scout, same as Finn.

Han Solo

Rogue (Scout)

Human, Smuggler, Chaotic Neutral

STR 10 DEX 18 CON 14 INT 12 WIS 12 CHA 14

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Athletics +3, Insight +7, Nature +7, Persuasion +7, Stealth +7, Survival +7

  • Feats: Alert, Lucky, Crossbow Expert

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: You’ll want a blaster and a cool looking utility belt. A hand crossbow will do just fine.

  • Skirmisher is definitely a good fit for Han.

  • Survivalist seems like it’s what he needs for smuggler skills.

 
 

Kylo Ren

I’ve been excited to talk about this one. Kylo Ren interestingly displays abilities from a wide variety of classes and subclasses. He’s an absolutely iconic oathbreaker (who kind of breaks his oath twice), which means that oathbreaker paladin is a good fit for him. As we did before, hexblade also gives him some great sith abilities. However, part of me so badly wants to give him levels of barbarian to truly display that his power comes from his strength and his rage. If you get far enough along and you want to take a one level dip in barbarian, go for it. I’ll back you.

Kylo Ren

Paladin (Oathbreaker)/Warlock (Hexblade)

Human, Knight, Lawful Evil

STR 18 DEX 12 CON 16 INT 10 WIS 10 CHA 14

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Athletics +6, Insight +3, Intimidation +5, Persuasion +5, Religion +3

  • Feats: Great Weapon Master, Tough

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: It’s sort of strange to imagine lightsabers as strength weapons, since they’re clearly based on dexterity in D&D terms. But Kylo Ren just fights with his like it’s a strength weapon. His greatsword-like lightsaber means that he obviously carries a greatsword.

  • Dreadful Aspect is a great display of Kylo Ren and how his enemies see him.

  • Divine Smite is perfect for those rage induced hits and crits that Kylo Ren makes.

  • Paladin Spells. Hellish rebuke, inflict wounds, branding smite, wrathful smite and zone of truth.

  • Warlock Spells. As with Maul and Vader, make your eldritch blast invisible and pick up mage hand and prestidigitation to represent manipulating the force. Also take cause fear, hex, witch bolt, wrathful smite, crown of madness, earthbind, shadow blade, shatter, and fear.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. The greatsword variant, if you can.

 
 

Princess Leia

There’s something about Princess Leia that make me really want to make her as a cleric. I think if I was just creating character builds in the spirit of Star Wars characters, I totally would. But to get a realistic snapshot of Leia Organa, I think we go with fighter. She’s obviously more of a ranged fighter and not a sword and shield fighter, but it fits. She does sometimes display force sensitivity and even masters her force powers in later movies but I don’t think she displays enough to warrant en eldritch knight build. If you want to take magic initiate to give her a few abilities, that could work.

Princess Leia

Fighter (Champion)

Human, Noble, Lawful Good

STR 10 DEX 18 CON 14 INT 12 WIS 14 CHA 12

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Insight +5, Nature +4, Persuasion +4, Stealth +7, Survival +5

  • Feats: Crossbow Expert, Sharpshooter

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Blasters or crossbows.

  • Archery Fighting Style to get the best ranged fighter you can.

  • Improved Critical is pretty straightforward.

 
 

Luke Skywalker

Luke Skywalker is another super classic D&D build. He’s a great archetypal hero and his adventures embody many of the tropes that D&D adventures often do. He’s the first Jedi Knight on the list that we’ll be tackling. Magic in 5e gets all of the force powers to come to life - though the Unearthed Arcana Mystic that came out a while back did a much better job at bringing a Jedi build to the table than any spellcasting class. I won’t even begin to make the claim that it’s balanced or that tables should use it, however. Jedi are kind of the wizards of the Star Wars mythos, though they aren’t wizards at all in 5th edition terms. To truly get a Jedi Knight correct, I think the best approach is to include a little bit of monk. Now, unfortunately for us that makes us pretty MAD, since the rest of what we’ll use is probably going to be warlock. But there is another option. The psychic warrior fighter subclass is the best fit for a Jedi Knight than anything we’ve ever seen in 5e (except mystic). I know I said official content only but psychic warrior is such a good fit that it’s worth trying to convince your dungeon master to let you use it. Plus, it may become official content soon anyways.

Luke Skywalker

Fighter (Psychic Warrior)

Human, Folk Hero, Lawful Good

STR 12 DEX 18 CON 16 INT 11 WIS 12 CHA 12

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +3, Athletics +4, Insight +4, Persuasion +5, Religion +3

  • Feats: Magic Initiate (Bard) - Pick up friends, minor illusion and charm person.

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: A lightsaber and blaster are two obvious necessities. If you can’t get either then a hand crossbow and a rapier will do.

  • Psionic Talent Options all of these are great displays of force mastery.

  • Telekinetic Adept is perfect for improved force mastery - like telekinesis.

  • Bulwark of Force. Damn it’s even named after the force. Different kind of force, I know.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. Even better if it once belonged to your father.

 
 

The Mandalorian (Din Djarin)

Mando is quite different from Boba Fett. For one, he’s much more adept at hand-to-hand combat than we currently know Boba Fett to be (comics and novels not included). He really feels like a paladin in the thematic ways and he even says “weapons are my religion” but he’s not a paladin in any mechanical ways. You technically could build him as a strength based ranger and I do love me some strength rangers. But I think the best fit we’ve got for him is eldritch knight. He doesn’t display any magic or arcane prowess but his spells can be reflavored to be of mechanical origin rather than arcane origin.

The Mandalorian

Fighter (Eldritch Knight)

Variant Human, Knight, Lawful Neutral

STR 18 DEX 12 CON 14 INT 14 WIS 12 CHA 12

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Athletics +7, Insight +4, Intimidation +4, Survival +4

  • Feats: Polearm Master, Sentinel.

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Any tool, gadget or gizmo you can get your hands on. A rifle or crossbow and a badass polearm weapon.

  • Fighting Style I’d go with archery on this one, just to make sure you’re good at ranged and melee combat.

  • Eldritch Knight Spells. Fire bolt, lightning lure, shocking grasp, true strike, burning hands, catapult, feather fall, fog cloud, grease, magic missile, shield, snare, darkvision, shatter - the list goes on and on.

Magic Items:

  • Boots of Levitation. Perfect for a jetpack.

 
 

Obi Wan Kenobi

Obi Wan isn’t all that different from Luke Skywalker, mechanically. From a roleplaying perspective he’s a very different character and you could really have fun playing him. But the Jedi Knights in general are mostly going to be built in the same way, especially if you’re using the Psychic Warrior. We’re going to use the same build for him as we used for Luke, though he definitely justifies more of the warlock force powers that we discussed earlier, as he tends to use them a lot more in combat, while Luke is much more hand-to-hand (at least in the OT).

Obi Wan Kenobi

Fighter (Psychic Warrior)

Human, Hermit, Lawful Good

STR 12 DEX 18 CON 16 INT 11 WIS 12 CHA 12

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +3, Athletics +4, Insight +4, Persuasion +5, Religion +3

  • Feats: Magic Initiate (Bard) - Pick up friends, minor illusion and charm person.

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Obi Wan isn’t fond of blasters. Rapiers or sabers only.

  • Psionic Talent Options all of these are great displays of force mastery.

  • Telekinetic Adept is perfect for improved force mastery - like telekinesis.

  • Bulwark of Force. Fitting for him just as it was for Luke.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. An elegant weapon for a more civilized age.

 
 

Palpatine (Darth Sidious)

Palpatine is a fun one to build. Somewhat similar to Vader, he doesn’t really “turn on” until at least level 9. Now, I know that Palpatine can totally hold his own in a lightsaber duel but the truth is - he doesn’t tend to go that route very often. Therefore I don’t feel the need to totally represent his combat potential on a 5e character sheet. He’ll get a little bit of martial prowess from hexblade, but he really needs some levels of sorcerer to go along with it. Sorlock is perfect for Palpy. After 3 levels of warlock, I’d go sorcerer so that you can pick up lightning bolt as soon as possible but it’s up to you when (or if) you want to go back and pick up a few more warlock levels.

Palpatine

Warlock (Hexblade)/Sorcerer (Shadow Magic)

Human, Courtier, Lawful Evil

STR 9 DEX 12 CON 14 INT 14 WIS 14 CHA 18

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +6, Insight +5, Intimidation +8, Persuasion +8

  • Feats: Tough, War Caster

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: The most important thing to you is the people in your control, not the items in your pocket.

  • Pact Boon I would actually go tomelock on this and pick up pact of the tome so you can pick up a few cantrips like chill touch and sapping sting, but it’s totally up to you.

  • Strength of the Grave is fitting for such a durable old sith lord.

  • Spells. Mage hand, lightning lure, shocking grasp, suggestion, charm person, disguise self, levitate, ray of sickness, false life, chaos bolt, witch bolt, lightning bolt. ALL the bolts.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. For when you want to face those pesky little Jedi hand-to-hand.

 
 

Poe Dameron

Poe is like Finn, in that he doesn’t necessarily display any extraordinary abilities. So like Finn, he makes an excellent rogue. Thief and scout could be used to build Poe but I particularly like mastermind for him.

Poe Dameron

Rogue (Mastermind)

Human, Soldier, Neutral Good

STR 10 DEX 18 CON 14 INT 12 WIS 12 CHA 14

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Athletics +3, Insight +7, Nature +7, Persuasion +7, Stealth +7, Survival +7

  • Feats: Lucky

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Nothing really noteworthy here except maybe picking up a blaster. A hand crossbow will do just fine.

  • Master of Intrigue this just seems so fitting to me.

  • Insightful Manipulator this is a Poe ability if I’ve ever seen one!

  • Misdirection is such a great skill for Poe. He uses misdirection while flying often.

 
 

Rey

Like Obi Wan and Luke Skywalker, I think psychic warrior is the way to go. Again, you just can’t beat it for a Jedi Knight build. Since she doesn’t wear any armor (nor do any of the other Jedi really) it would be advisable to build her as a dexterity based character, though I understand the desire to build her as a strength character so that she can use a polearm. If you’re considering a different class for character creation and want to do the warlock/monk build, Rey definitely gets lots of monk levels. With the warlock/monk build you can also not worry about having strength to use your polearm, since you can get warlock to let you use charisma for it anyways.

Rey

Fighter (Psychic Warrior)

Human, Hermit, Lawful Good

STR 12 DEX 18 CON 16 INT 11 WIS 12 CHA 12

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Arcana +3, Athletics +4, Insight +4, Persuasion +5, Religion +3

  • Feats: Magic Initiate (Bard) - Pick up friends, minor illusion and charm person.

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: Polearms and blasters.

  • Psionic Talent Options all of these are great displays of force mastery.

  • Telekinetic Adept is perfect for improved force mastery - like telekinesis.

  • Bulwark of Force. Fitting for her just as it was for the others.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. Please make it yellow!

 
 

Yoda

I debated how to build this for quite a while. Some time ago I did build yoda as a monk/sorcerer and I thought it fit very well. But with the addition of the psychic warrior fighter, I had to reconsider how to tackle him. I think you could do fighter/sorcerer but I want to try something a little off the wall. I’m going to go with fighter/divination wizard. I think divination wizard lets yoda get some wizard spells that really fit the Jedi Knight ethos, and also gives him a few flavorful divination abilities. Funny enough, the divination wizard’s best ability (portent) is also the least fitting for Yoda, though it isn’t entirely unfitting if you flavor it correctly.

Yoda

Fighter (Psychic Warrior)/Wizard (Divination)

Ghostwise Halfling, Far Traveler, Lawful Good

STR 8 DEX 18 CON 12 INT 16 WIS 13 CHA 10

Features & Traits:

  • Skills: Acrobatics +6, Arcana +5, Insight +4, Perception +4, Religion +5

  • Feats: Mobile, Keen Mind, Observant

Notable Features:

  • Equipment: A nice little walking stick and a tiny little lightsaber (or rapier).

  • Portent can be reflavored as reflexive force usage - sort of like cutting words.

  • The Third Eye is a divination feature that is very fitting for Yoda.

  • Spells. Mage hand, shape water, true strike, catapult, expeditious retreat, jump, longstrider, shield, detect thoughts, levitate, locate object, clairvoyance, arcane eye, legend lore, true seeing. Hell, I would even give yoda counterspell if it was up to me.

Magic Items:

  • Sunblade. Can’t be one of the greatest swordsmen in the galaxy without it!

A few years ago, it was actually much harder to try to build these Star Wars characters for 5th edition Dungeons and Dragons than it is today. I tried doing it before Xanathar’s Guide To Everything came out and the options were lacking to say the least. Almost everyone who was a force user had to be a terrible hodgepodge of monk/sorcerer or just mystic, which almost no table allowed. Ever since the release of the hexblade and the new psychic warrior fighter, the job is a whole lot easier and you can get a whole lot more accurate. As you can see, pretty much everyone is just fighters, rogues or warlocks now, which is great.

Gone are the days when people tell you that Star Wars can’t be done in 5th Edition or that D&D isn’t the correct system for it. Based on the builds you’ve seen here today, I think you realize that it just simply isn’t true anymore. As more content is released for 5e and character creation becomes more flexible, you can get closer and closer to building your favorite fantasy characters.
