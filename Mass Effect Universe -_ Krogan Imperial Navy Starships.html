A note on Krogan computer networking:
What is denoted by a "non-networked computer system" is not an entirely segregated system, but it serves as a fail-safe against potential cyber warfare attacks. Computers are minimally networked for coordination during operations of the ship. If a hack or virus is detected in a section of the ship, crew are able to immediately cut the network connection to prevent hackers (or a virus) from accessing other computers on the ship.

Description:
Arising after the introduction of RKM technology, Mirah Zhenya quickly called for a CIWS frigate to fill the gap left in ship defenses by the new weapon, helping to design it as well. These frigates, would surround the larger ships of a fleet, shifting as needed and providing as much CIWS fire as possible to protect them from conventional and RKM missiles, as well as snub craft like fighters and bombers.The ships also pack standard MAC cannons in addition to their CIWS guns with their primary role in contributing to the flak field created by larger vessels. In an absolute pinch, these cannons can be used offensively, but that would be a last resort.

Given their role as CIWS vessels, these frigates completely lack any spinal armament. They also feature a high end targeting VI, running calculations for creation of flak fields, leading CIWS guns on tracking a target. Like other ships, the computers on these frigates aren’t networked, providing protection from potential cyber warfare attacks. These are not the fanciest frigates, but they’ll do what they need to do.

Description:
The Hammerhead-class frigate is an interesting ship design, masquerading deception in what appears to be a bridge within the recess of the vessel’s prow. In reality, it’s an ingenious disguise behind a retractable door concealing its main armament. The CIC rests deep within the rear of the vessel, protecting those steering the ship. This frigate was one Mirah Zhenya fully designed for a Krogan crew, allowing the Krogan to easily modify the vessel and begin producing them.

Typical of the former government’s frigates, this ship can be used offensively or defensively, swapping between the role of a defensive picket ship or interceptor as well as that of a light assault ship in conjunction with a fleet. In this way, it’s essentially a slightly more powerful version of the Shield-class, packing more power on the prow and broadside and with dorsal and ventral mounted CIWS.

What makes the Hammerhead unique compared to the Shield-class is the bulkier fuselage. It allows the installation of dedicated computer systems to take part in Electronic Warfare operations. While the ships may be outpowered compared to larger ships, these dedicated computers play a large role and are more than effective against pirates or slavers, or a single spliced together pirate cruiser. This makes the Hammerhead valuable to keep around in its multiple roles.

Description:
The Shield-class Frigate was a light frigate of Krogan design meant for use as a dedicated ship for battle rather than relying on the troop transports for use in combat. Though, with a small spinal gun as the main armament, there was only so much the ship could do in an offensive role aside from light fire support with the fleet. The primary role of this ship, after Mirah Zhenya started reorganizing ships, was to play defensively.

The Shield very much gets its name from the reconfiguration, meant as a defensive picket ship. While the Sickle-class would interdict enemy vessels, these ships played two roles: joining their CIWS in protecting the fleet, but turning their main guns on enemy vessels when they get too close to the fleet for comfort. In this defensive picket role, they form part of the shield of the fleet, along with the more recent purpose-built Akora-class CIWS Frigates.

Offensively, the Shield is still used as intended, a light fire support ship in the use of the spinal gun while moving in with the rest of the fleet. It still carries its defensive role at close range, intercepting enemy missiles and snub craft as needed with broadside CIWS batteries. However, it doesn’t mean the frigate can’t join in pummeling the enemy with its main armament if needed. It may be a bit of a multirole, but at least it’s suited to the roles it plays rather than trying to play to too many.

The Sickle-class was one of Mirah Zhenya's first functional frigate vessel designs for a new navy. While not accepted at home, it was more than welcome in the Krogan Empire. The blueprints for the hull made their way to EGA. EGA’s designers went from there. Developing the ship to be roomier for Krogan, the Sickle was brought into existence as a Krogan frigate.

Geared towards a defensive role as a picket ship, the Sickle is meant to engage enemy vessels who get too close to the fleet and utilize their guns to provide a flak screen in conjunction with the larger vessels of the fleet. When the fleet moves offensively, these ships fire their 100m spinal guns for light fire support along with the larger prow mounted guns of the heavier vessels. Their turrets then work offensively and fire at enemy ships from close range.

Typical of all the frigates, these are not meant to operate solo in any fashion, nor should they ever.

Description:
Skirting the line of a light cruiser size vessel, the Lira-Class Pocket Carrier was a design that was never sold, enhanced by the Krogan in 2203, and put into their own service as part of the new doctrine. Repurposed as an escort carrier and recon vessel, the Lira serves an interesting role in support of a fleet in one way or another. The ship can also be utilized in the support of Special Forces missions given the impressive sensor array of the ship, capable of conducting scans while in low orbit.

The Lira is a nimble vessel, a shipwide RCS grid giving it mobility in combat, even maintaining speed and mobility after the loss of a single engine nacelle during testing. The interior of the ship is lacking comforts in many respects, with tough confines by Krogan standards. Exposed piping runs throughout the ship and condensation issues have forced the installation of dehumidifiers to prevent problems with technological issues. The ship also lacks munition creation facilities, leaving the Lira depending on resupply from the fleet to maintain its fighters.



The ship has 12 medium MAC cannons with twin barrels, cannons that can be used offensively with standard slugs or defensively in the creation of a small flak field. The vessel is further defended by a CIWS system against fighters and conventional armaments as well as an anti-RKM CIWS system. The most offensive weapon is a VLS system containing 6 anti-ship Hailstorm missiles.

Interestingly, unlike the largest ships of the class where the CIC also doubles as the bridge deep within the interior of the ship, the Lira-class has an exposed bridge. This gives the crew a great vantage point over the action and great visibility. The major downside is, of course, it’s rather prominently exposed and thus at risk of targeting. While there is an emergency secondary bridge within Engineering, this has minimal sensors and is only meant to get the ship back home.

In an attack role, these destroyers would attack by using their main cannons in a charge before changing to broadsides and circling their target.

Devoid of any launch tubes for fighters or any shuttles, the ship has external docking points to receive shuttles from other ships. Truly geared towards a primarily defensive mission, but with some armaments for assault, the Korlus-class shows yet another piece of a doctrine that was in development for decades.

Description:
The Revolution-class really implied what this class once was: a revolution in shipbuilding for Mirah Zhenya. She was planned as a fearsome hunter-killer, an indigenous, independently developed IES Cruiser. Such a role is reflected in her profile, her weaponry, engine design, even systems like a thermal power system once designed to use stored heat to produce power for the vessel and increase energy efficiency, and possibly prolong time between venting heat into space. Such a dream would never come to be, but it did not mean the ship would die.

The Turian Hierarchy reviewed the designs when the Krogan Imperial Navy petitioned to have her constructed in full IES configuration. The Turians were impressed with Zhenya's ingenuity, but denied the use of IES systems, save for the thermal powerplants. After some redesigning of the interior, the Revolution was approved for construction and the would-be IES Cruiser was brought into existence as a semi-stealthy cruiser.



Even without the IES systems, the ship still packed enough of her features to make use of passive stealth, even if it would be visible on thermal scopes. She can linger in wait, scouting a system for enemy movement before leaving and reporting information. Given this, she was also not meant as an assault craft, as shown by the existence of only 6 magnetic launch tubes, meant to launch stealth fighters. While a hindrance in conventional fleet operations, it’s not something that can’t be overcome.

In terms of guns, she carries more hardware per square meter than even the Nerpa and Victoria. A bristling gunship if there ever was one. Aside from 8 chin mounted “spinal” guns, the main offensive weapon are heavy MAC cannons which are also capable of firing anti-fighter missiles if needed. These MAC cannons are also capable of making use of fighter-grade RKMs, something that would make this ship all the more fearsome in an IES configuration. In addition to some smaller cannons, which can act as defensive flak guns if the situation is dire or as anti-ship offensive weapons, the ship also carries a bristling CIWS system and 6 VLS missile tubes.



Though she’s more than capable of operating alone if required, this was with two factors in mind: IES and a lack of RKM technology. With both of these gone, the class has lost much of this capability, but she can still prove a capable diversion. A single Revolution-class cruiser can still survive for up to a year and a half without resupply and can lead a hostile pursuer on a chase across multiple systems, easily capable of leading them into a trap. The cruisers are sometimes still used for Special Forces insertion missions of required.

Though not yet tested in combat, the Krogan Imperial Navy is looking to see if they got what they paid for out of these cruisers.

Quickly designed to fill a niche, The Krantt-class was designed as an escort ship meant to be built in limited numbers. Drawing from the advantages provided from the typical design of flight pods and a main body, the designers at Empire General Assembly set to work developing a cruiser that can take over military escort duties for Nerpa-class Battlecruisers as part of a task force. Paired with the Battlemaster-class Light Battlecarriers, this class acts as a lighter support vessel, bringing its heavy dorsal and prow-mounted firepower against an opponent.

Somewhat more poorly suited to a conventional fleet engagement, in a larger setting, this ship’s guns are more than welcome, but in its task force configuration, would be poorly outmatched by a conventional fleet. These ships are far more geared to deal with pirates in their standard duties. Like the Battlemaster, these ships are meant to assault an enemy. The Dai-class was a concept, a concept that never made it to the drawing board previously. The Krogan were more than happy to pick it up and run with it. Already anticipating a lack of their Victoria-class Super Battlecarriers due to expense and the inability to operate many Nerpa-class Battlecarriers, the Empire was already looking into an alternative design.

Drawing on elements from both the Revolution and Victoria-classes, the idea of the class was to make a dedicated fleet carrier at somewhat less expense. Similar to the Nerpa-class, her computers are not networked completely, saving the ship from a catastrophic cyber attack in the event of a hack. Her guns, taking an offensive posture in defending itself, can still prove a horrific opponent until relief arrives.

Being a carrier, and unlike the Victoria-class, this ship was not meant to operate solo by any means. In case of a separation, the best bet for survival would be to plot course back to friendly lines before the enemy can catch up. Overall, she’s suited for her duties, and post-Akuze, the only authorized new-construction carrier class. The class have been constructed with RKM shielding and had point defense retrofitted in 2212.

The Krogan loved the concept, a lethal ship that did not just sit back as a carrier. They went right after it, after it was suitable for Krogan with a roomier interior, of course. A ship suitable for Asari does not a good Krogan vessel make. But Zhenya was more than happy to do it, seeing her design come to life in the Navy.

Coming in as some of the first dedicated carriers of the Empire, the Nerpa-class is the first dreadnought length vessel to utilize flight pods in her design as dedicated hangar bays, capable of docking shuttles and even Corvette and small Frigate-sized vessels within her flight pods. Docking a ship in a flight pod causes an inherent weakness in combat operations that fighters have to maneuver around it or shift activity to the other pod. These pods also have to be retracted prior to any FTL travel, due to the size of the eezo cores installed on the vessel. Failure to do alters the mass of the flight pods to become super dense, shearing off the vessel and causing massive damage to infrastructure connecting the pods to the main hull. This is a weakness corrected in later designs utilizing flight pods.



Like her more advanced counterpart, the Victoria-class, the Nerpa-class has offensive bite fitting in the very offensive naval doctrine she was designed for. She’s much weaker in terms of guns than dreadnoughts and, while she packs more of them, her forward guns lack the barrel length of even the Emperor-class. Obviously, the class makes up for this in fighter compliments and gunships, acting as a carrier. But the blistering gun batteries tell their own story on lethality.

After Akuze, the ships were retrofitted with new systems developed as countermeasures to the RKM weapons introduced by the Atticans. The massive size of the class makes them extremely vulnerable to these missiles. Additionally, Akuze’s revelations cancelled any new construction of this class, leaving the seven in service the only massive carriers of this class in existence, though Mirah may override the cancellations if the new countermeasures function as intended. The class has been retrofitted with shielding and CIWS defenses to counter RKM weaponry. All the same, pirates should avoid poking a ship at their own peril.

Description:
Designed prior to the Reaper War, though never constructed until recently, this ship represents the cutting edge of what might have been. However, this is still the cutting edge of something that became a reality in the end. Taking lessons learned in the Reaper War and improving it further only made it better. The largest and most sophisticated warship in service of the Krogan Empire, the ship is also a single class vessel due to the expense, an expense the Empire had hoped would be well worth it. In only her appearance over Tuchanka for the first time, she’s already made a fine impression.

The Krogan Empire desired to have their own dedicated carrier, problem was that there was no carrier out there that could fulfill a combat role and launch fighters. The Victoria-class is, in the end, that desire fulfilled. Defended by her flak fields during an offensive, she can also charge in with other vessels, using her prow’s guns to lethal effect before turning to broadside and bringing her automated flak batteries in an offensive role, easily partaking in the doctrine of delivering a death by a thousand cuts.



Unlike most ships, the class features a small observation deck at the prow. The ship itself is commanded from the Combat Information Center (CIC) which doubles as the bridge. The ship thus heavily relies on sensors, visual reports from pilots, and visual reports from observation areas throughout the ship. A massive improvement over the Nerpa-class design, the class features flight pods that have no need to retract before an FTL jump thanks to strengthening of the design. This allows the Victoria-class to immediately launch fighters after leaving FTL and giving her a combat ready stature from the get go.

The ship’s construction was substantially delayed after the Battle of Akuze and the introduction of Relativistic Kill Missiles in the galaxy. In fact, it was halted for a short time to give time for countermeasures to develop. The most recent of which were added on to fresh CIWS mounts for point defense. The most interesting feature are her dorsal-ventral flight pods, which must rotate a fighter on lifts before entering the pressurized hangar area of the flight pods. While this is a finicky system requiring constant maintenance, it is well worth it to double fighter capacity.



The ship was designed, in a worst case scenario, to operate alone if cut off from her fleet, given the longevity that her systems offer. This changed with the introduction of the RKM weapons that now force the ship, if separated, to immediately plot a course for friendly space or risk destruction. While she may have been a fearsome contender before, she needs her fleet now more than ever. Some of these ideas of longevity are seen in her foundries and ore-processing systems that allow for the production of new fighters, gunships, firearms, and even down to munitions. This is great for survivability alone, and even providing tactical resupply in her fleet.

With a long history, finally coming to fruition, the Krogan are looking forward to what this ship brings them in her service to the Empire.
