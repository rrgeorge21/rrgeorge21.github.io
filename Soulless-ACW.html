Attacks, techniques and abilities:

Echo is the name the Prophet gave the hero. Echo is a shadow of his former self. A soulless shell. An empty vessel that wants to be filled. The Elder Scrolls foretold the rise of a hero who would save Tamriel from the Daedric threat and other dangers.

  • Soulless - A mortal kidnapped at the moment of murder from Mundus, his soul was torn out by Mannimarco and stolen by Molag Bal. The Echo was originally intended to be the Daedric Prince's weapon, but as a hero of prophecy, he thwarted the Lord of Subjugation's plans. In exchange for his real body, the Soulless received an imprint that allows him to create an imitation of the Azure Plasma in Coldharbour like a Daedra. Normally, the bodies of Soul-Shriven are defective because they have lost the focusing principle of their Anuic souls and their echoes form a flawed image. However, despite all the side effects and drawbacks, the Echo was able to transform his essence so that he became a perfect Soul-Shriven, without any flaws. After the Echo returned the soul from Molag Bal, it remained to exist outside the mortal plane, independent of the body.
  • Regeneration - The Soulless One has restored its physical form with the help of a celestial shard. In doing so, it has become linked to both Nirn and Oblivion. By absorbing a piece of the mortal plane's essence, the Echo has acquired a modified body. Soul-Shriven, despite the loss of their soul, unlike Daedra, possess some other natural Anuic aspect, that is, information about the mortal essence. Moreover, when such a Soul-Shriven has a high enough Anuic significance, upon contact with Padomaic creatia (the substance of the plane of Oblivion), its body is formed almost instantly. A perfect Soul-Shriven has a flawless body in Coldharbour, that is, a full copy of the body it had in Mundus during life.
    • Basis for Restoration - The Soulless restore their body on the mortal plane by possessing soul gems and connecting to Tamriel's wayshrines. These are a network of portals that serve as conduits to Oblivion and Aetherius, and as such contain both Aedric and Daedric magic. Rather than being banished to Coldharbour as a Daedra, the Soulless can restore themselves at the nearest wayshrine or at the same place they died, with a soul gem in reserve. In order to learn how to quickly travel between wayshrines and be attuned to them, the mortal's soul must be outside of Mundus.
  • Elder Scrolls Rewriting - The Elder Scrolls provide insight into the structure of reality itself, their prophecies are a living, viscous entity. The Soulless can rewrite them through their actions, making the prophecies inconsistent at certain points in history. The Elder Scrolls can still point to some purpose for the Echo, but it has no power to determine or predict its own fate.

The Prisoner is a "mechanism" or "loophole". Appearing out of nowhere, born under unknown stars and unknown parents, an extremely powerful mythical figure in the Aurbis. The Eight Limbs (Aedra) and their Lost Ninth ( Lorkhan ) always, always make sure that the loophole is found. Sometimes to their own detriment, but it is a sure bet to ensure the survival of the current kalpa.

  • Ensuring a stable reality - mortals are servants who perform tasks to maintain order in Aurbis. Every plowed field, every child born, or every war that occurs maintains the stability of reality according to the creators' plan. Itelia serves as a conduit for the emergence of Prisoners. Thus, the Soulless repeats the accomplishment of the Chief Archon and is somehow connected to this Magne-Ge.
  • Development Potential – The Prisoner is able to develop himself and his abilities to such an extent as to guarantee the survival of the current kalpa. The gods provide him with all the necessary opportunities. The Prisoner has the power to turn reality into a metaphor.
  • Uncertain Fate - The Prisoner's essence is beyond all known possibilities that Aios, designed by Sotha Sil to determine all possible events that threaten the Clockwork City, can read, and the Prophet perceives the Prisoner as a wound in the body of time, a rupture in reality that should not be and cannot exist for long.
  • Understanding the Universe – The Prisoner realizes the reality of his imprisonment, he sees the walls that limit him, the chains of causality that bind him to its course. The Prisoner sees the door to his cage, looks through the bars and perceives that which exists beyond causality. Beyond time. Therefore, he can escape.
Magic:

Magic is a fundamental energy that can be transformed into various forms for any purpose. Magic comes from Aetherius (the world of the Gods), and it gets to Mundus (the world of people) through the rifts in Oblivion: the stars and the Sun. Magic is a gift from the Gods, and therefore, all mortals have the ability to control it, and change reality, even if locally, and perhaps temporarily, but the change will be real.

School of Destruction - the splitting of material connections under the direct influence of the magician's power is characteristic of this school of magic. Includes the effects of elemental strikes and disintegration, as well as magic that absorbs attributes and characteristics. Additionally contains spells of various types of damage, weakening, destruction of weapons and armor.

  • Natural elements - electricity, like flame and cold - are expressions of magical power that originate from the natural elements. The reality of Mundus is a great tapestry woven from matter and magic, an electricity spell directs magic through the fabric and warp of the tapestry, stirring its fibers, causing sparks that coalesce into magical lightning. Spells based on other elements are used in a similar manner.
  • Body Enhancement - By passing elemental energies through one's own body, the caster can increase one's abilities. For example, destructive magical energy eradicates disease and increases the pain threshold, and by exposing oneself to a lightning discharge, the mage deepens the understanding of the natural forces of magic. Passing through the body, magical energy accumulates in the muscles and fills the body with a power far exceeding that given to it by nature.
  • Weakening the enemy - vulnerability spells make the target more susceptible to various types of damage. This can be an increase in vulnerability to elemental, magical, physical damage, poison and disease. In this way, you can cause a weakening of the body, deterioration of physical condition, nausea, dizziness and other effects.
  • Effect on matter - due to destructive magical energy, the caster can influence matter in various ways, using it both to turn victims into stone or any objects, and to disintegrate enemies.
  • Absorption - Absorption spells allow the mage to absorb not only the life and magical energy of the victim, but also affect the physical characteristics, attributes of the target, the level of mastery of one of the skills, experience and luck.
  • Aetheric magic - "falling stars" are particles of matter and magic that come from either Oblivion or Aetherius, which sometimes move through space. Aetheric magic allows them to be manifested in Nirn as spells.
  • Organ Damage - The caster is able to turn the victim's internal organs into acid that corrodes even stones, which ultimately leads to the complete destruction of the body.
  • Accelerated Perception is a spell that replaces the body's own energy with magical energy, amplifying the mage's impulses with magic and allowing him to see the world in slow motion.
  • Equipment Destruction - These spells degrade the condition of enemy weapons and armor, including enchanted ones, causing instant rusting and splitting of materials.

School of Restoration - uses magic that reflects damage and restores connections in damaged areas of matter. Studies the use of amulets, healing, curing curses and poisoning, resistance to physical damage, and also allows you to banish the dead (enhanced cleansing spells).

  • Healing - Healing spells restore integrity by reshaping damaged material, healing the body from fatal wounds, including damage to internal organs. Healing magic can restore stolen abilities, skills, experience, physical characteristics, and personal attributes.
  • Resistance - these spells increase resistance to physical and magical damage, prevent the effects of paralysis, absorb enemy spells and replenish their own reserves of magical energy, and also increase the body's resistance to poisons and diseases.
  • Cleansing - Cleansing magic repels, banishes, and kills the undead. The caster surrounds themselves with a special aura of light that causes the undead to keep their distance or flee. Offensive cleansing spells can completely burn the dead.
  • Barriers - the caster uses magical energy to create a barrier that absorbs or blocks physical and magical damage. In addition to continuously maintained external barriers, the mage is able to impose protection on his own body.
  • Increase Energy - by directing streams of healing magical energy into one's own body, the caster greatly increases the level of vital, magical energy and endurance.

The School of Alteration – distorts the surrounding reality by directly imposing the will of the mage. Studies spells of paralysis, water breathing and walking on water, opening and closing locks, as well as creating protective elemental shields – for example, elemental armor.

  • Gravity Control - the mage is able to control the forces of gravity to make his body weightless, increase the enemy's load or reduce the weight of carried objects, as well as increase the height and length of jumps, slow down the speed of free fall or move objects over a significant distance.
  • Shields - are divided into several types of protection, due to which the magician is able to block normal attacks, can make himself more difficult to hit by surrounding himself with a destructive elemental aura, or protect himself from adverse environmental conditions.
  • Auxiliary Magic - Alteration spells allow the use of such abilities as: breathing underwater, moving on the surface of water, steep slopes and other surfaces, and also increase the speed of movement underwater.
  • Opening/Closing - the magic of change makes it possible to open not only locks on chests and doors, but also to seal some portals.
  • Light – Light magic creates magical illumination and is the simplest. The magician forms a beam of directed light and sends it to the desired point, where it illuminates the desired area.

School of Mysticism – uses a class of spells that change the very nature of magic. Includes such abilities as: dispersing or absorbing not only the spell, but also the magical energy that fuels it, allowing you to trap souls, use teleportation, and detect the presence of someone nearby.

  • Teleportation - the ability to instantly move anywhere, including between dimensions, there is also a return spell that transfers the magician to a place previously marked with a special sign. This also includes the art of creating portals that help to move to certain coordinates in space.
  • Reflection - The mage turns the air around him into a reflective shield, or uses more complex magic that reflects and redirects spells aimed at him.
  • Dispel - Allows the caster to remove all side effects from curses based on magical energy and to nullify various magical effects.
  • Telepathy – experienced magicians have the ability to mentally transmit messages to other people, being thousands of kilometers away.
  • Spiritual trap - by tearing souls out of creatures, the magician is able to imprison it in a special stone, thus, the entity bound to the soul stone will be trapped and will not be able to get out of it.
  • Telekinesis - the ability allows you to move objects at a distance, depending on the amount of energy invested, the weight of the object that the magician can influence increases.
  • Detection - a spell aimed at detecting all nearby objects, allowing you to determine the exact location of any creature, any object or other desired target.

School of Illusions - illusion spells alter one's own or someone else's perception of reality. This school of magic studies spells that affect the willpower and mind of beings, conceal presence, inspire various emotions, as well as spells that control sound and light.

  • Effect on vision - some spells help the magician navigate at night as well as in daylight, and also impose opposite effects on the enemy, ranging from slight darkening of vision to complete blindness.
  • Chameleon - the spell makes enemies perceive the mage as something that blends into the environment and generally resembles the effect of invisibility, but at the same time blocks the ability of particularly sensitive creatures to find the location of the mage.
  • Sound Control - This ability allows the mage to control sound, for example, by locally suppressing it to move almost silently, or by influencing someone by causing a disorienting noise in their head.
  • Empathy - these spells are aimed at changing the perception of enemies by instilling various feelings and emotions, auxiliary magic increases morale and encourages allies.
  • Clairvoyance is a spell that shows the magician the way to a specific goal, especially useful in dungeons and labyrinths, helping to get out of them by the shortest route.
  • Silence - the spell deprives the enemy of the ability to use magic and cast any spells, even if their use does not require the pronunciation of words.
  • Material illusions - by instilling certain thoughts into the enemy, it is possible to transform him into some object or subject if he believes in it.
  • Paralysis - the mage is able to impose paralysis effects on a specific target both from a distance and upon contact at close range.

The School of Witchcraft - practices spells to summon Daedra from Oblivion and spirits from Aetherius, allows the exorcism of summoned entities and the binding of them into various weapons and armor. Includes spells to raise the dead, forbidden rituals, and necromancy.

  • Daedric Equipment - Lesser creatures from Oblivion can be summoned and bound into the form of weapons or armor until their shells are destroyed or they are banished, items made from Daedric essence are examples of short-term bonds.

Magic craft - is divided into several professions. Workplaces differ depending on the task. For example, an alchemist needs an alchemical laboratory, an enchanter needs a special magic table with a pentagram of souls and special stones, and to create spells nothing specific is required, only scrolls and runes and a quiet place.

  • Blacksmithing is the skill of making heavy armor and bladed weapons from metals and magical components. All the nuances and secrets of this craft require the same deep understanding as in any magical art, and only over time, through research and practice, can one become a true master of his craft.
  • Provisioning – skills in preparing food and drinks. A hearty meal fills the stomach, increasing overall well-being – physical and spiritual, allowing you to replenish your vitality, magical energy and reserves of strength. Strong drinks improve your mood, allowing you to recover faster.
  • Tailoring is the crafting of light and medium armor using tanning, weaving, and sewing. A tailor can use materials that have magical properties to create durable armor with multiple layers of magical protection.
  • Enchantment is the art of casting spells on various objects. When working on a pentagram, the process of moving the soul from special stones to the object occurs, thus, you can give the desired thing certain magical properties.
  • Carpentry - Similar to blacksmithing, only the raw materials for creating equipment and the resources needed to improve items differ. Carpenters create shields, bows, and various staves with magical properties.
  • Alchemy is the skill of creating potions and poisons by extracting magical properties from common substances. There are many types of potions and poisons, and their effects vary greatly depending on the ingredients used.
  • Spellcrafting is a creative approach where the mage combines several different properties to create a new, more powerful spell based on the basic one.

Non-systemic magic - there are several magical directions that cannot be classified and over time have not been organized or included in a specific school of magic.

  • Taurmaturgy - the spells of this long-forgotten school of magic use effects that do not change the nature or properties of a substance, but are capable of temporarily influencing an object within the laws of nature.
  • Auramancy - allows you to awaken and view memories by influencing the aura of objects where the owner's emotional imprint was left.
  • Flesh Magic - In general, the rituals of this magic resemble necromancy, but to create a vile Daedric creature, body parts of dismembered creatures are used.
  • Spiritual Magic - focuses on manipulating souls and Imprints trapped in Oblivion, allowing one to destroy, absorb, seal, and use soul energy for attack.
  • Prediction is an ability that powerful magicians possess; entering a state of concentration, they receive information about the future through mystical visions.
  • Chronomancy - some magicians are able to locally control time, for example: slow it down, speed it up, stop it or travel through time.
  • Weather Magic – allows you to control the weather on various scales, causing both local destructive weather phenomena and changing the climate of an entire region.

Destiny Stones - located throughout Tamriel, these ancient stones are magical in nature, a source of immense power, and are linked to the thirteen constellations. Touching a stone can grant you power and rewrite your destiny. Constellations bring good fortune and a sign from above, changing the future. Only one blessing is available, but with certain equipment, you can get two at once.

  • Lady - her time is the month of Hearth Fire. Lady is a sign of unbending will and inexhaustible health. Those born under this sign are kind and have great patience. When the Lady shines, she smiles at the sick, heals them and rewards those who bravely overcome difficulties.
  • The Thief is the last guardian constellation. Its time is the dark month of the Evening Star. Those born under the sign of the Thief are not necessarily thieves, but they are more likely to be thieves and are rarely caught. They will sometimes feel a lack of luck, so they live shorter lives than those born under other signs.
  • Atronach - his season is the month of Sun's Dusk. Those born under this sign are natural wizards with a large reserve of magical energy, but they cannot replenish this reserve on their own.
  • Tower - its season is the month of the Beginning of Frosts. Those born under the sign of the Tower have a talent for finding gold, and can also open various locks.
  • The Apprentice – The Apprentice season is the month of the High Sun. Those born under this sign are predisposed to magic of any kind, but also more vulnerable to it. They are characterized by impulsiveness and pride due to their connection with Magnus.
  • The Warrior is the first guardian constellation, it protects its subjects during their seasons. Those born under the sign of the Warrior are good at all types of weapons, but have a hot-tempered nature.
  • The Serpent is a sign that travels across the sky. It has no season. Those born under this sign can be blessed more than others, and cursed, but they have a talent for quick healing.
  • Horse - its season is the month of Mid-Year. Those born under this sign have great endurance and speed, but are very impatient and always in a hurry. The Horse accelerates the subject's progress towards his destiny.
  • Shadow - the season of the Shadow - the month of the Second Seed. It gives those born under its sign the ability to hide in the shadows. This sign is associated with the Void and represents the unknown, making its subjects cold-blooded, patient and cautious.
  • The Magician is a guardian constellation whose season is the month of Rain's Hand, when magic was first used by humans. Those born under the sign of the Magician have a predisposition to magic, but are often arrogant and absent-minded.
  • Lover - his season is the month of Sunrise. Those born under this sign are very attractive and passionate, few can resist the charm of the Lover.
  • Lord - his season is the month of First Seed. Those born under the sign of the Lord are stronger and healthier than those born under other signs. His subjects are strong, calculating and ambitious.
  • Ritual - its season is the month of the Morning Star. Those born under this sign have different abilities depending on the position of the moons and gods. The ritual is associated with Magnus and grants healing to its subjects.

The Sorcerer is a caster of arcane magic who can cast lightning and summon Daedra from Oblivion. The Sorcerer specializes in dark spells that weaken enemies.

  • Shock Magic - There are many ways to use shock magic, you can shock enemies directly with electricity, but also use other variations, for example: the caster can wrap himself in an aura of lightning, which will deflect some physical and magical attacks, while simultaneously damaging nearby enemies. Lightning can be cast on any surface in the form of a rune, which will explode upon contact or after a certain time. The sorcerer can direct an electric bolt at a nearby target, and then instantly "ride the lightning" and move to this location.
  • Daedric Magic - Oblivion energy is used by the sorcerer to encase an enemy in a sphere that suppresses magic and blocks the ability to cast spells. The sorcerer can summon crystal projectiles from Oblivion and barbed mines that explode on contact.
  • Support Magic - the sorcerer is able to pass elemental energy through his body, increasing protection from spells and resistance to magical effects, and using Daedric magic, he can create armor and surround the body with an impenetrable protective field.
  • Summoning Daedra - the mage binds Daedra to his world through pacts and rituals, most servants are summoned by the sorcerer only for a very short period, on a thin and fragile line between coercion and connection.
  • Dark Magic - This discipline allows you to neutralize, drain other wizards, feed on their magic and strength, and also reproduces some of the dangerous effects of spells cast by Daedra.

The Dragon Knight is a master of weapons who uses the ancient Akaviri tradition of fighting through the power of the spirit. The Dragon Knight's destructive magic physically alters the world around him. Children of Mundus draw strength from the earth and the energy of dragons.

  • Dragon Magic - Using ancient magic, the Dragon Knight can set enemies on fire, pull them towards him with a fiery lasso, wrap himself in a fiery cloak, cover his weapons in flames, and even breathe fire like a real dragon.
  • Earth Power - The Dragon Knight is connected to Mundus, the source of his abilities is a piece of the essence of the Heart of Lorkhan, which he calls upon during battle, its energy permeates his entire body.
  • Support spells - the Dragon Knight's magic allows him to strengthen his body with scales, making it stronger than metal, create stone shields, breathe out clouds of suffocating ash and poison, immobilize enemies, turning them into stone statues.
  • Lava Manipulation - Calling upon magma from the depths of Nirn, the Dragon Knight cloaks himself in armor made of lava and molten metal, and attacks enemies with eruptions from beneath the earth.
  • Dragon Blood - The Dragon Knight is imbued with the energy of dragons in the surrounding area, allowing him to heal wounds, increase magic resistance, reflect spells, create explosions, and stun enemies.

Templar - Wielder of the power of cleansing light and scorching sun, the Templar draws on the infinite power of Magnus to destroy enemies and heal allies. The righteous magic of the wandering knight is especially effective against necromancy and the creatures of Oblivion. Templar orders are spread throughout Tamriel, so they worship different deities and wield modified magic, such as based on the power of Stendarr .

  • Energy Manipulation - Magnus' magic is used by the knight to summon fiery gouts and spear-like beams of solar energy from space to rain down on the ground and vaporize enemies.
  • Healing - Aedric energy heals the body in the presence of mortal wounds, poison, diseases, removes negative magical effects and pacifies enslaved souls with the help of its holy radiance.
  • Rituals of Purification - The holy light of Stendarr is especially effective not only in destroying Daedra and undead, but also in protecting the knight's mind, body and soul from necromantic spells.
  • Barriers - The divine light of Stendarr surrounds the Templar in a dissipating aura that weakens enemy attacks and takes the almost tangible form of a shield of protection that reflects spells.
  • Magic weapons - the knight's arsenal includes weapons of light: swords, axes and throwing spears. They have various effects, which allows them to inflict not only magical damage, but also stun, disorient and weaken enemies.

The Nightblade is a silent assassin who relies on cunning, stealth, agility, speed, and luck. He specializes in attacking from the shadows, absorbing the life force of his victims, and escaping by disappearing into the void.

  • Shadow Passage - Among the Nightblade's magical spells, there is none more useful than the ability to move quickly. Over time, casting the spell becomes almost reflexive. The key to mastering it is the ability to "blend in" by cloaking oneself in the shadows of all creatures and objects in the Aurbis. Of course, these are not the true shadows cast by an opaque object blocking the light, but their emanation at the limit of each object's ability creates the full impression of their real existence in the Mundus. This requires the ability to focus on the hyperagonal perception of the flow of magic passing through the caster. Once the Nightblade can "feel" a local transpontine deformation, transliminal leaping to any point within the reach zone becomes almost trivial.
  • Shadow Absorption - Nightblade uses absorption spells that transfer life force and energy from the victim to the caster. Magical absorption of life is related to instantaneous translocation spells, as it creates a transliminal flow of essence from the target to the caster. Using hyperagonal sense of magic, Nightblade perceives the transpontine deformation of the target and "pierces" it, and by interrupting the flow, absorbs the essence lost by the target. Thus, instead of "stepping through the shadows", as in normal translocation, the mage performs a "shadow absorption" from one point in space to another.
  • Darkness Manipulation - Nightblade creates a small shadow area around itself that absorbs most damage, as well as slowing the speed of enemies and dealing darkness damage.
  • Evasion - The Nightblade creates a ghostly aura around itself, projecting a blurred image into the attacker's mind, thereby increasing the chance of avoiding an enemy's attack or spell.
  • Defense - Shadows can be absorbed to strengthen the mind, or combined to create shadow blades.
  • Healing Suppression – This ability reduces the effectiveness of healing spells.
  • Acceleration - the spell increases the speed of attack and movement.
  • Shadow Rune - instills fear and destroys the souls of nearby enemies.
  • Slow - the spell slows down the enemy's attack and movement speed.
  • Invisibility - the spell removes invisibility, the ability to hide in the shadows and dissolve into the void.
  • Clone - The Nightblade can create a double of itself from the shadows.

The Keeper is a master of flora and fauna, a protector of the Green, and a master storyteller whose tales of nature become magical realities. The Keeper draws power from nature itself, allowing him to control the wild beasts of Tamriel, bathe his allies in healing plants, and harness destructive elemental energies.

  • Power of Nature - Like the Wood Elf priests, the Keepers are closely tied to Y'ffre, the deity of song and the forest. They use the natural environment to alter and reshape reality itself. Where the Tellers use a kind of illusionary magic, the Keepers' abilities lie more in the realm of what would traditionally be called alteration or sorcery.
  • Beastly Helpers – The Keeper can summon a beast companion in battle to attack enemies. Wild beasts include bears, cliff racers, netches, and giant stinging flies. Due to the Keeper's close connection to nature, he can also control other beasts found in Tamriel.
  • Medicinal Plants – The Guardian grows a healing forest around himself, where all kinds of trees, flowers, plants and mushrooms with healing properties are found. The power of nature heals wounds, removes poisons from the body and eradicates diseases.
  • Frost Spells - The Guardian's offensive magic is based on the control of ice, snow and wind, allowing them to create ice spears, unleash devastating frost storms on their enemies, wrap themselves in a cloak of snow, erect barriers of ice and freeze their enemies.
  • Portal - By summoning an ancient portal under the enemy's feet, after a while the Guardian can teleport it to himself and freeze it with an ice rune.

Necromancer - all the dead and undead of Tamriel are a resource used by the master of the dark arts. The necromancer is capable of reviving corpses, death magic and healing spells. Black spells allow you to manipulate the souls of the dead, spiritual energies and their bodies, most of the necromantic knowledge comes from the Daedric forces. At its core, necromancy is the binding of the soul to a form prepared or, in some cases, specially made by the caster. That is, souls bound in this way are imprisoned against their will and have no chance of release. The sorcerer can bestow consciousness on souls occupying structures chosen for them.

  • General characteristics of the undead - the undead are divided into the raised, the returned, the damned and those representing disgusting hybrids. Each of them requires special conditions for raising and control.
    • Returned - ghosts, spirits, and wraiths are incarnated for a variety of reasons: some are bound to Nirn by powerful curses, others are summoned through rituals, and others are unable or unwilling to leave due to unfinished business. There are families that are bound by their own ancestors.
    • The Damned - Undead are not always the result of renegade mages' malevolent actions upon souls and rotting flesh. Malevolent afflictions such as noxiphilic sanguivoria can corrupt the living as well. The result is an undead body that requires the blood of the living to survive.
    • Vile Hybrids - Some undead defy easy classification. For example, a lich is a corpse that has self-resurrected using the soul that inhabited it in life. This state is usually only achieved by powerful spellcasters seeking immortality.
    • Raised - These undead are created when a necromancer summons and infuses an enslaved spirit into a corpse or body construct. Raised come in many forms, from mundane skeletons to massive flesh atronachs.
  • Raise Dead - A powerful master of necromancy can raise long-dead subjects, but most practitioners of this art require a subject that has been dead for no more than three days. Attempting to raise minions without proper knowledge and training in the art of necromancy can result in an incomplete soul binding. The subsequent disruption of the relationship between master and minion will be terrible for the hapless aspiring necromancer.
    • Subordination - a living dead who can speak cannot but be powerful, it exists only to absorb and devour the energy of the living. However, a necromancer who has sufficiently strong magic can grant it intelligence after death. Corpses raised by necromantic spells become absolutely obedient and ready to carry out any orders. The more experienced the magician, the stronger the connection between him and the reanimated dead.
    • Change of appearance - All powerful mages have illusion spells and can change their appearance, and so do the living dead. A harmless-looking ghost of a person or any other creature may be an undead in disguise.
  • Animal Animation - The art of necromancy can be practiced on animals, but few experiments yield positive results. A minion's ability to follow its master's orders is limited by the subject's level of intelligence in life. Raising the body of a human, elf, or beastman can make an excellent servant, but an animal raised is at best a guard dog. Reanimated animals are sometimes unable to distinguish their master from other living beings, and many practitioners have been torn apart by the animals they raised.
  • Summoning entities from Aetherius - to perform the ritual, it is necessary to prepare a circular area with a diameter of at least three steps; outline the circle with a mixture of one part chalk, one part salt and one part ash; prepare your ritual tools; write the name of the spirit that the necromancer wants to summon on a piece of animal skin. After desecrating the circle, you need to light candles in a certain order, take the animus geode and release its power, mentally holding the image of the deceased. Once the caster has summoned the spirit, he will be bound to the animus geode; the geode will act as a kind of signal fire, allowing you to call the bound shadow to yourself at will.
  • Soul Capture – the ability to trap the souls of creatures in a phylactery of a suitable size or in morpholiths such as soul stones. The essence inside the stone can then be used to recharge and enchant magical items. However, only the rare black soul stone can contain the soul of a higher being, such as a human or an elf. The souls of primitive creatures are trapped in other stones – they can be of different colors, but they are all considered to be white soul stones. Hence the division of all souls into black and white. White souls are less dangerous than black ones, but also not as powerful. If the size of the stone and the soul trapped in it do not match, then the stone can capture particles of the magician's soul if he happens to touch the stone.
  • Support Magic - A master of the dark arts uses all manner of methods to support himself and his allies in battle. By summoning fragments of shattered souls, the necromancer transforms them into healing energy that heals wounds, or creates impenetrable barriers, forming a protective area around himself. Raised dead and controlled undead can be shrouded in cold, fire, or lightning to hurl themselves at enemies.
  • Resurrection of allies - the necromancer draws on the powers of life and death, so he can resurrect and reanimate not only the undead or the dead, but also his fallen brothers in battle, without causing harm to their bodies and souls. Necromancy spells allow you to prolong and support the life of allies in battle, as well as impose enhancements to increase stamina.

The Arcanist is a servant of Hermaeus Mora who uses powerful runes, black books, tomes of ancient knowledge, and the supernatural energies of the Apocrypha. Drawing on forbidden knowledge and the power of the Daedric Prince, the Arcanist creates destructive spells, nigh-impenetrable matrices of protective spells, and uses healing charms to restore.

  • Spell Conduits - The Arcanist's Black Books are not just books in the literal sense, they are fragments of supernatural knowledge from the Apocrypha in the user's mind that are embodied in this form, as the structure of the Apocrypha is metaphorically a library, as it is the dimension where all knowledge is collected.
    • Energy Beams - By tearing apart the fabric of Aurbis, the Arcanist summons the scions of Hermaeus Mora from Oblivion. These creatures unleash homing energy beams on all nearby enemies, destroying reality. The Arcanist transforms the pure knowledge of Apocrypha into beams that distort reality in the world around them.
    • Defensive Magic – Fate is a highly honed tool in the Arcanist’s arsenal. By warping the fabric of fate, he adjusts the odds and luck of his surroundings, avoiding enemy attacks. By summoning runic armor or protective tentacles from the Abyssal Sea, the Arcanist creates a shield around himself that absorbs damage from attacks and spells.
    • Runic Charms - Using ancient runes, the Arcanist summons various weapons, creates magical explosions, reflects and redirects attacks, imposes negative and infinite effects on enemies that weaken the mind, lower the skills and characteristics of targets, and also changes the course of fate so that the enemy cannot defend himself from spells.
    • Hermaeus Mora's Patronage - The Daedric Prince aids his followers when they need protection. The arcanist gains the ability to heal from injuries and additional resistance that protects his body, mind, and soul from the negative influence of enemies.
    • Gates of Apocrypha - This spell allows the Arcanist to destroy the wall between worlds and open a portal in the chosen location, a copy of which appears right in front of him. While the portal is open, the mage can move between them, entering one and exiting another. By calling upon words of power, the Arcanist gains the ability to travel between worlds on the battlefield.

Psijic Order - This ancient organization originated in Summerset and laid the foundations for the magical arts as we know them today. The Psijic Order can be credited with many things, including the creation of the Mages Guild and the development of the School of Mysticism. Mysticism, or the Old Ways, was believed to be able to unlock the secrets of the universe when properly practiced. It could also be used to peer into the future, which may have been the basis for the advice the Psijics gave to the rulers of Tamriel. The Soulless One is attuned to Artaeum and is connected to the Psijic Order, so he will always be able to travel to the island, no matter where it is.

  • The Path of the Ancients is a philosophy of self-knowledge and meditation, with which one can bend the forces of nature to one's will. It is very different from magic, but its effect is much the same. The Path of the Ancients combines Mysticism, the Eleven Powers, and the divine laws that bind and translate the fabric of reality into philosophy and life. The Psijics have the deepest knowledge of the Ehlnofey, also known as the Bones of the Earth, who were once the Aedra.

Psijic Endeavor – Veloth describes the Psijic Endeavor as a process of glorious apotheosis, where time bends inward and outward into a form that is always new. Those who are able to achieve this state experience an indescribable sense of divinity, and become free of the limitations of creation.

  • Wisdom is the foundation that determines the level of ability and potential for development of the Psijics. They are interested in studying the fundamental foundations of the world and the universe, what holds it together, and what can threaten it. This study has allowed the Psijics to develop knowledge and power that is not found anywhere else in Tamriel, as well as the ability to manipulate space and time.
    • Insight - A power bestowed upon the Soulless by the Psijic Order, allowing the Soulless to see into the spirit world. The Echo can interact with rifts throughout Tamriel and close rifts in space and time.
    • Ally Healing - By invoking the Rites of Moawita, Psijic replaces his normal and power attacks with healing abilities that can only be used on allies.
    • Return - the ability allows you to step into the past to a certain point, returning the Psijic's state to its original one.
    • Freeze – The Psijic freezes the flow of time in a targeted area, blocking enemy healing abilities.
    • Acceleration - By warping space and time around him, the Psijic increases his movement speed.
    • Enchant - The Psijic imbues his weapon with magical power, releasing elemental energy upon impact.
    • Sphere is a spell that releases energy discharges from the body.
    • Meditation – the state of meditation allows you to heal your body and mind.
    • Barrier - A shield that absorbs physical and magical damage directed at the Psijic.
    • Suppression is the ability to reduce damage taken from spells.

The Fighters Guild provides work for mercenaries, volunteers, and ensures that contracts are concluded with the local population. Citizens can conclude contracts with the guild to destroy parasites and monsters, deliver goods on dangerous routes, collect animals for arenas, and perform other tasks as decided by the Guild Administration. Strong and healthy people are accepted to serve in the Guild. A candidate must have skills in handling long swords, axes, blunt weapons, and shields. Guild members must be able to wear heavy armor.

  • Dawn's Radiance - This spell summons Meridia's sacred sword, which has tremendous penetrating power.
  • Rune of Protection - by drawing a protective rune on the ground, the fighter heals himself and repels enemies.
  • Dawnguard Crossbow - Shoots silver bolts to hit and pull in enemies.
  • Revealing – The fighter reveals invisible and hidden enemies around him.
  • Trap - launches a trap with a sharp blade in the selected location, which is activated after a while.
  • Torchbearer - The fighter summons an enchanted torch for illumination or as a weapon.

Mages Guild – Provides employment to those skilled in magic and sets laws regarding the proper use of magic. The Guild is dedicated to collecting, preserving, and disseminating magical knowledge for the benefit of all citizens of Tamriel. The Mages Guild accepts only candidates with highly developed intellects and strong wills. Candidates must demonstrate mastery of the major schools of magic: Destruction, Alteration, Illusion, and Mysticism. Candidates must also demonstrate practical skills in Enchanting and Alchemy.

  • Summon Celestial Body - The spell summons a comet from the constellations, which falls on enemies and explodes.
  • Light - The mage summons a clot of magical light, revealing hidden and invisible enemies around.
  • Immobilize - the spell binds the enemy with chaos magic and restricts his movements.
  • Ulfsilda's Caution - The mage charges himself with Ulfsilda's magical runes, which cause a magical explosion around him.
  • Rune - The spell draws a rune of cosmic flame on the ground, which explodes on contact.
  • Energy Conversion - The mage makes a deal with the forces of Oblivion, exchanging life force for magical energy.

Dark Brotherhood - The Dark Brotherhood's roots lie in the religious cult known as the Morag Tong, which split off in the Second Era. The Morag Tong worshiped the Daedric Prince Mephala, who encouraged their ritualistic killings. They remain largely unseen, but are easily found by those willing to pay for their services.

  • Blade of Woe - This spell summons a legendary weapon of the Dark Brotherhood, which allows you to deliver a deadly stealth strike to an unsuspecting victim.
  • Inconspicuousness is a feature that allows an assassin to not arouse suspicion in others even in the case of a murder in front of witnesses, or to practically not arouse aggression in monsters while riding a horse.
  • Escape - There is a chance that when using the Blade of Woe, the assassin can be enveloped in a veil of stealth, allowing them to escape the scene of the murder unnoticed.
  • Haste - The Assassin gains a magical effect that significantly increases movement speed after killing an enemy with the Blade of Woe.

The Thieves' Guild is what is called a crime regulator, a guild is more than just a gang of criminals. However, you can't just show up at your local guild building. The Thieves' Guild has no official building. But thieves like to hang out where their friends live. In the guild, you can become agile and fast, learn to sneak up on people, pick locks and disarm traps, and use light melee and throwing weapons.

  • Oblivion is an ability that reduces suspicion and the possibility of identifying a thief, even if he committed a crime in front of witnesses. The radius of detection by guards decreases, they practically do not notice his actions.   • Caches - Thieves' caches are hidden throughout Tamriel, filled with items and equipment needed to conduct their activities. Only members of the Thieves Guild can open them.

The Dauntless are a guild of adventurers who explore dungeons, caves, and ancient ruins. They come looking for an adrenaline rush or a worthy way to pass on to the next world. The Dauntless tirelessly hone their skills, which are designed for group adventures. They have a high degree of insight, but spend their lives in taverns and are often under the influence of drink.

  • Blood Manipulation - The adventurer sacrifices his life force and creates a fountain of blood, which, when it hits enemies, imposes a special magical effect that grants healing to the entire group of Dauntless.

  • Emotion Instillation – The Dauntless' ability to kindle the flames of hatred in the hearts of their enemies. An ally who attacks a provoked enemy can activate the spell and set their enemies on fire.
  • Bone Manipulation – The adventurer surrounds himself with a vortex of bones, gaining a shield that absorbs and reflects magic. A nearby Dauntless can activate the synergy and surround the entire party with a barrier.
  • Sphere – The spell creates a sphere of destruction that slowly flies forward and deals damage to enemies with destructive energy. An ally near the sphere can activate an explosion that restores magicka and stamina.
  • Synergy is a magical power that unites and binds the entire group of Dauntless, with its help healing and other useful magical effects are transmitted to all adventurers at once.

  • Blood control - vampires are able to control the movement of blood in the bodies of creatures from a distance, and can also absorb it, completely drying out the victim and healing themselves. At the same time, the blood of vampires has healing properties, it heals deadly diseases and removes poisons from the human body.
  • Night vision - vampires see at night as well as during the day, another feature of their vision is the ability to see the aura of living beings.
  • Intangibility - the ability of vampires to become intangible allows them to pass through objects, move in a misty form, and dissolve into the darkness.
  • Regeneration - vampires have regeneration, due to which they almost instantly recover from injuries that would be fatal for an ordinary person.
  • Resistance - Pureblood vampires have supernatural protection, they are immune to disease, poison, cold, paralysis and invulnerable to conventional weapons.

  • Invisibility - the ability of a vampire to become invisible to normal vision and detection spells, allowing the observer to perceive him as something that blends into the environment.

  • Intimidation - Pureblood vampires exude a terrifying aura that causes those around them to become paralyzed and instills a chilling fear in nearby creatures.

  • Coldharbour Bond - Vampire souls are directly bound to Molag Bal, and upon death they will enter Oblivion and spend eternity in Coldharbour.

  • Blood Weapon - The Vampire Lord has the ability to form weapons from his own blood or the blood he absorbs from his opponents.

Lycanthropy is a disease, curse, or blessing of Hircine that turns a person into a half-beast. There are seven forms of lycanthropy in Tamriel, though "lycanthrope" in the narrow sense of the word refers to werewolves who transform into wolves. Followers of Hircine can ritually bestow this gift upon someone else.

  • Werewolf Physiology - Werewolves tear out chunks of flesh with their sharp fangs, and their claws act as naturally enchanted weapons that penetrate the defenses of war golems, atronachs, and other creatures that are immune to mundane weapons. Werewolf hides provide excellent protection against bladed weapons, arrows, and spells.
  • Superhuman Physical Stats - Werewolves possess strength superior to that of humans, allowing them to tear enemies apart with their bare hands, carry enormous weights, and travel long distances without feeling fatigue.
  • Full Moon - During the full moon of Masser or Secunda, werewolves gain special powers that greatly increase their physical capabilities.
  • Superhuman senses - the werewolf has keen hearing and a keen sense of smell, with which he can track his prey, and he is also able to see the aura of living beings and observe them from a great distance.
  • Eerie Howl - The werewolf's howl instills fear in those around him and allows him to call upon pack members for help, both converted and normal wolves and bears.
  • Regeneration - Werewolves have the ability to regenerate wounds and restore life energy through the Gift of Hircine or by devouring the corpses of people and creatures.
  • Festering Lupinus - Carrying a disease that turns creatures into lycanthropes, a werewolf can infect them with it through a bite or cut on their body.

  • Link to the Hunting Grounds - Lycanthropes' souls are linked to Hircine, who is their creator. Upon death, they enter Oblivion and will spend eternity in his dimension, subject to the incessant thirst for blood and the hunt.
